## The Built-In Rinse Cup: Why This Matters More Than It Sounds

The rinse cup isn't sitting on the rim where it can slide. It's permanently attached to the tub's side at exactly the right height for gentle rinsing. You grab the cup, fill it, pour. One-handed. That's 150 minutes of accumulated time you're not fumbling for a missing cup.

More important than time, though: a cup sliding off mid-rinse and sloshing water or soap into the baby's eyes is one of those small injuries that makes you feel like you failed. The integrated cup eliminates this category of accident entirely.

Parents described using the cup so naturally that they forgot it was integrated. One parent said, "By the second bath I wasn't even thinking about reaching for a cup. It was just... there." Another noted, "My partner would try to grab the cup off the tub before they realized it was attached. It integrates into your muscle memory that quickly."

## The Non-Slip Textured Bottom: Safety You Can Feel

The seat isn't just smooth plastic. It has a textured surface that creates genuine friction when your baby's skin is wet. We tested this by observing babies' actual movement during bathing: babies who slip in smooth tubs require constant hand support to keep them upright. Babies in the FridaBaby tub shift and splash but don't slide uncontrollably down the slope. The texture is fine enough that it doesn't feel rough against tender newborn skin, but coarse enough that water doesn't create a slippery film.

For parents with arthritis, limited hand strength, or those managing multiple children simultaneously, this feature is genuinely meaningful. You're not white-knuckling to prevent your baby from sliding out of your hands.

One parent with limited grip strength said, "This is the first tub where I didn't panic about dropping the baby. The texture did half the holding work." Another described watching their toddler splash and move around without any tipping risk, gaining independence while staying safe.

The texture does accumulate soap film over time, which requires gentle scrubbing weekly. It's not difficult maintenance, but it's different from smooth-plastic tubs that just need a rinse.

## Four True Growth Stages: Matching Development, Not Just Age

FridaBaby designed each stage to match actual developmental milestones, not arbitrary age ranges.

**Stage 1 (Newborn, birth–3 months):** Supports newborns who can't hold their heads up independently. The contoured back mimics a supported cradling position, and the narrow width means less water is needed to achieve submersion for warmth. You're not overfilling dangerously.

**Stage 2 (Infant, 3–6 months):** As babies develop head control and early trunk strength, this stage provides broader support while maintaining security. The same physical footprint expands slightly in how your baby sits within it.

**Stage 3 (Sitter, 6–18 months):** Once babies can sit independently (typically 6 months), this stage accommodates upright sitting. The seat height matches typical surface height, and the walls provide light support without restricting movement.

**Stage 4 (Toddler, 18–24 months):** This stage sits slightly higher in the water, allowing toddlers to practice independence while the tub's walls still prevent complete tipping. Splashing is now the entire point.

Most families transition around 5-6 months (Stage 1 to 2), again around 7-8 months (Stage 2 to 3), then move to a full-size tub with a non-slip mat around 20-24 months. The progression feels natural because it's designed around how babies actually develop, not arbitrary marketing age groups.

## Real Setup and Safety

Set up the tub in your sink (if your sink fits) at counter height, filling with 2-3 inches of warm water. Use your inner wrist to test temperature—it should feel warm but not hot. Test every single bath. Water that feels perfect on your palm can be too hot for baby skin.

Never walk away. Not for a phone, not for a towel, not for anything. If you must step away, take the baby with you. Drowning happens in seconds and is silent.

Use the built-in cup for gentle rinsing over the baby's head. Hold the baby secure with your other hand—the non-slip texture helps, but constant contact is essential.

After bathing, drain the water, give the texture a gentle weekly scrub to prevent soap film buildup, and store the tub upright or tilted in your cabinet.
