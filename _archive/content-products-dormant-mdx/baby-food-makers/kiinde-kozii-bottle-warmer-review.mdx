Most water bath warmers can overshoot this threshold, destroying the very components that make breast milk medically superior to formula.

## Understanding Breast Milk Nutrition: Why Temperature Matters

Breast milk is often described as "liquid gold," and the description isn't just sentiment. Unlike formula, breast milk is alive — it contains white blood cells, antibodies, and beneficial bacteria that actively protect your baby. This biological activity is sensitive to temperature.

**Optimal temperature ranges:**
- Below 104°F (40°C): All immunological components remain active
- 104-108°F: Antibodies begin degrading, live cells start dying
- Above 108°F: Significant loss of protective proteins
- Above 140°F: Most nutritional and immune benefits are destroyed

The Kozii specifically targets the 104°F threshold because this is where you preserve the most benefit while still delivering warm milk that feels comforting to your baby. Water bath warmers, by contrast, rely on guesswork with water temperature, often overshooting into the 110-120°F range where meaningful nutrient loss occurs.

## The Mist Heating Technology

Traditional water bath warmers (the plastic basket of hot water you sink a bottle into) work through convection — surrounding heat gradually warms the bottle's exterior. The problem: if the water is too hot, the outer layer of milk overheats while the interior remains cool. Uneven heating + high temperatures = damaged nutrients.

The Kozii uses warm-water mist, which distributes heat more evenly across the bottle's surface. It's more like a gentle steam room than a bath. The result is more consistent temperature distribution and better control over the maximum reached temperature. The Kozii's mechanism is also simple enough that it rarely fails — no electronic thermostats, just a reliable heating element with a timed shutoff.
