At $479, it sits squarely in mid-range crib territory — significantly more than budget options like the DaVinci Kalani 4-in-1, meaningfully less than premium cribs from brands like Pottery Barn Kids or RH Baby. The Hudson occupies that middle ground with a combination of design quality, safety certification, and build durability that few competitors match at the price.

## First Impressions: The Design Holds Up

The Hudson's most immediately recognizable feature is its spindle side rails — clean, evenly spaced vertical bars with a slightly tapered profile that reads as modern without being stark. It's available in White, Washed Natural, Cane (a warm wheat tone), Slate, and a few additional finishes depending on the retailer.

The white finish has been the most popular since the product launched, and it photographs well in nursery setups — which matters because parents who are researching this product are often building a nursery aesthetic, not just buying a box to sleep in. That said, the Washed Natural finish shows less of the scuff and mark accumulation that happens when toddlers start treating the crib rails as chew toys and crash bars simultaneously.

The profile of the crib is rectangular and clean. No scrollwork, no curved headboard, no ornamental elements. This restraint is what makes it work in a wide range of nursery styles — it doesn't force a design direction, it accommodates them. An all-white nursery, a Scandinavian minimal setup, a neutral-toned boho nursery: the Hudson fits all three without looking like a compromise.

At 53.75 inches long by 29.5 inches wide, the Hudson's footprint is standard. It's not a compact crib and won't fit comfortably in a very small room — plan for clearance on three sides for safe access. The 35-inch height sits at a typical crib height that allows most adults to lay a sleeping baby into it without excessive reaching.

## Safety Certifications: What GREENGUARD Gold Actually Means

The Hudson carries GREENGUARD Gold certification, which is the primary safety claim on most Babyletto marketing. Understanding what this certification covers helps evaluate whether it's worth the premium over an uncertified product.

GREENGUARD certification, administered by UL (Underwriters Laboratories), tests products for chemical emissions — specifically volatile organic compounds (VOCs). Paints, finishes, adhesives, and composite wood materials can all off-gas VOCs into the surrounding air. In a nursery, where a baby spends 12–16 hours per day in close proximity to the furniture, VOC emissions are a legitimate concern.

GREENGUARD Gold is the stricter of the two GREENGUARD tiers. It applies the same VOC limits used for schools and healthcare facilities — environments where occupants are considered sensitive. Regular GREENGUARD certification applies general standards; Gold applies standards calibrated for children.

For the Hudson specifically, this means the paint, finish, and any adhesives used in the solid pine construction have been independently tested and certified to emit below the GREENGUARD Gold thresholds. Babyletto also uses sustainable New Zealand pine rather than MDF or particleboard in the main structural components, which reduces the adhesive binder concern that comes with engineered wood products.

The crib also meets ASTM F1169 (Standard Consumer Safety Specification for Full-Size Baby Cribs) and CPSC (Consumer Product Safety Commission) regulations. ASTM F1169 specifies maximum slat spacing of 2-3/8 inches (6cm) — a dimension set to prevent head entrapment. The Hudson's slat spacing meets this standard. It's JPMA (Juvenile Products Manufacturers Association) certified as well.

This combination — GREENGUARD Gold, ASTM F1169, CPSC, JPMA — represents the full safety certification suite for a crib. The DaVinci Kalani 4-in-1 also carries GREENGUARD Gold and JPMA certification, which is worth noting: both cribs hit the same safety benchmarks, and the Hudson's price premium is not about superior safety certification.

## Build Quality: Solid Pine at This Price Point

The Hudson uses solid New Zealand pine for the main structural components — the end panels, the spindle rails, and the support structure. Pine is a softwood, which means it's lighter than hardwood alternatives and more susceptible to denting and scratching. This is the honest tradeoff for a sustainable, certified-emission pine crib versus a hardwood alternative at a higher price.

In practical terms: the crib feels solid during assembly and in use. There's no wobble when the mattress support is at the lowest position. The slat rails don't flex under hand pressure. For a baby or a toddler using the conversion configuration, it's appropriately sturdy.

For a toddler who treats their crib as a bouncing platform — and many do — the pine will accumulate dents and marks over time. This is true of any softwood furniture. If your nursery aesthetic depends on the crib maintaining an immaculate appearance through age 5, a hardwood crib at a higher price point will hold up better cosmetically. If functional durability matters more than cosmetic preservation, the Hudson is fine.

The hardware is metal and well-machined. The mattress support has four height positions, and adjusting between them requires a screwdriver but is straightforward. Most parents set the mattress at the highest position for newborns (easier to reach into the crib) and lower it once the baby can pull up to standing — typically around 8–10 months.

## The 3-in-1 Conversion System

The Hudson converts through three configurations: crib, toddler bed, and daybed. The toddler rail is included in the box, which is meaningfully different from the DaVinci Kalani's approach of selling conversion rails separately.

**Crib configuration**: Standard use from birth through roughly age 2–3, when the baby can climb out. The drop side was removed from cribs sold in the U.S. after CPSC safety rule changes in 2011; the Hudson's fixed sides are standard for all cribs on the market today.

**Toddler bed configuration**: Remove the front rail (the lower panel), install the included toddler guard rail, and the Hudson becomes a low toddler bed. The crib mattress continues in this configuration — you don't need a new mattress. The toddler rail spans one side and has an opening for the child to get in and out. This configuration works from roughly age 2 through 5.

**Daybed configuration**: Remove the toddler rail, leave the back panel and end panels. The crib becomes an open-sided daybed, useful for an older child's room or a guest daybed. This is the least-used configuration in practice — most families either sell the furniture before this stage or have moved to a twin bed.

The conversion kit sequence is logical, and the included toddler rail is a genuine cost advantage. With the DaVinci Kalani, you pay separately for the toddler rail ($30–40) and the full-size rail kit ($60–80). With the Hudson, the toddler rail is included, but the Hudson doesn't offer full-size bed conversion at all — that's a 3-in-1, not a 4-in-1. If your intent is to use the same bed frame through full-size, the DaVinci Kalani is the better choice. If you know you'll transition to a twin or full-size bed purchase when the child is ready, the Hudson's included toddler rail is a straightforward value.

## Assembly: Budget an Afternoon

Assembly takes 60 to 90 minutes for most adults working alone, longer with an infant in the house demanding attention. The instructions are printed clearly but assume some familiarity with furniture assembly. The hardware is organized and labeled, which helps, but the instruction diagrams compress several steps into single illustrations that require careful reading.

The most common assembly frustration reported by Hudson owners: aligning the slat rail panels with the end panel attachment points. The holes are precisely machined, which means there's little tolerance for misalignment — you need to get the angle exactly right before the bolts will thread cleanly. Taking your time on the initial alignment saves significant frustration.

Tools needed: a standard Phillips screwdriver and a flat-head screwdriver are included in the box. An electric screwdriver makes the process significantly faster. A second set of hands is helpful for holding panels in position during initial bolt placement but is not strictly required.

## The No-Storage Criticism

The Hudson has no under-crib storage drawer, and this is a real gap if you're furnishing a small nursery. Under-crib space is valuable real estate in a small room — a drawer can hold extra sheets, sleep sacks, swaddles, and other frequently accessed items without requiring a separate dresser.

The DaVinci Kalani 4-in-1 solves this with an optional under-crib drawer available as a separate purchase. The Babyletto Scoot (another Babyletto model) also includes a drawer. The Hudson simply does not offer this option.

If under-crib storage is a priority, the Hudson is the wrong product for your situation. A crib with an integrated drawer at a similar or slightly higher price — or the Kalani with the drawer add-on — will serve a small nursery significantly better.

If storage isn't a constraint, the clean visual profile of the Hudson without a drawer is actually an aesthetic advantage in some nursery designs.

## The Separate Mattress Cost: Factor It In

The Hudson's $479 price does not include a mattress. A standard crib mattress (28 x 52 inches) is required separately. Budget mattresses start around $50–60; the mattresses most commonly recommended with the Hudson — the Newton Baby Crib Mattress and the Naturepedic Organic Crib Mattress — run $150–350.

If you pair the Hudson with the Newton Baby (approximately $200) or the Naturepedic Organic ($300), your total investment is $679–$779. That's meaningfully more than the headline $479 price, and worth factoring into budget comparisons. The DaVinci Kalani at $279 plus the same Newton mattress totals approximately $479 — the same as the Hudson alone.

The mattress choice matters for safe sleep. The AAP recommends a firm, flat mattress with no softer toppers or pads. The Newton Baby Crib Mattress is a particularly strong recommendation because its 100% breathable core design allows airflow even if a baby ends up face-down — an important safety feature. The Naturepedic is GOTS certified organic and firm, making it the preferred pairing if organic materials are a priority throughout the nursery setup.

## How It Compares to the DaVinci Kalani 4-in-1

The DaVinci Kalani 4-in-1 is made by Million Dollar Baby — the same parent company as Babyletto. Both cribs carry GREENGUARD Gold certification. Both use pine construction. Both meet ASTM F1169 and CPSC requirements.

The differences are meaningful:

**Price**: Kalani at $279 vs. Hudson at $479. That's a $200 difference, or roughly 70% more for the Hudson.

**Conversion scope**: The Kalani converts through 4 configurations including full-size bed; the Hudson tops out at daybed. If you're planning to use this frame as a full-size bed when the child is 8–10 years old, only the Kalani delivers.

**Storage**: The Kalani has an optional under-crib drawer; the Hudson doesn't.

**Design**: The Hudson's modern spindle profile is aesthetically cleaner and more distinctive. The Kalani's design is traditional and pleasant but not particularly noteworthy. If nursery aesthetics are driving the decision, the Hudson is the better-looking product.

**Included extras**: The Hudson includes the toddler rail; the Kalani's toddler rail is sold separately. At the Kalani's lower price, this matters — factor in the rail purchase cost when comparing total prices.

The bottom line comparison: the DaVinci Kalani is the smarter financial choice for families who prioritize cost and long-term convertibility. The Babyletto Hudson is worth the premium for families who care about design, want the toddler rail included, and don't plan to use the frame as a full-size bed.

**vs. Nanit Pro integration**: The Nanit Pro camera is designed to mount on the crib rail and track baby breathing and sleep patterns. The Hudson's straight, fixed rail profile is compatible with standard Nanit mounting hardware without modification. If you're pairing with smart monitoring, the clean rail profile of the Hudson is an advantage over cribs with decorative top rail elements.

**vs. SNOO Smart Sleeper**: The SNOO is a different category — a motorized bassinet for the first 6 months, not a crib. Many families use the SNOO for the newborn phase and then transition to the Hudson for the crib phase. They're complementary products, not competitors. If you're considering the SNOO, plan to purchase the Hudson (or a similar crib) separately for the transition to a larger sleep space around 5–6 months.

## Who Should Buy the Babyletto Hudson 3-in-1

Buy the Hudson if you're prioritizing nursery aesthetics alongside safety, you want the toddler rail included rather than an add-on purchase, and you're comfortable with the $479 price knowing you'll also need a mattress. It's the crib to buy when you want something that looks as good in photographs as it does in person, and that will hold up through the toddler bed phase without feeling like a compromise at any stage.

Consider the DaVinci Kalani instead if budget is a primary concern, you need under-crib storage, or you plan to use the frame as a full-size bed years down the road.

The Hudson is our top pick in the mid-range convertible crib category because it succeeds at the things it promises: design quality, safety certification, solid construction, and a conversion path that covers the most-used stages of early childhood furniture.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What mattress fits the Babyletto Hudson?**
Any standard crib mattress (28 x 52 inches) fits the Hudson. We recommend the Newton Baby Crib Mattress for its 100% breathable core design, or the Naturepedic Organic Crib Mattress if certified organic materials are a priority. Both are firm, flat, and appropriately sized for the Hudson frame.

**Does the Babyletto Hudson convert to a full-size bed?**
No — the Hudson converts to a toddler bed (using the included toddler rail) and then a daybed. It does not offer full-size or twin bed conversion. For a crib that converts to a full-size bed, look at the DaVinci Kalani 4-in-1 or the Babyletto Scoot 3-in-1, which offers different conversion options.
