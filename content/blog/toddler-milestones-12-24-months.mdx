 # Toddler Milestones by Month: What to Expect from 12-24 Months 🧸🎉

Welcome to another exciting chapter in your parenting journey! As your little one grows, they'll be sprouting new skills and abilities every month. Here's a comprehensive guide on what to expect during the fascinating 12-24 months period, referencing expert guidance from organizations like NHS, WHO, AAP, and CDC.

## 🌱 12 Months: From Crawler to Cruiser

At 12 months, your toddler is transitioning from crawling to cruising (walking while holding onto furniture). They should also be able to understand simple commands like "no" and may start to use gestures like waving goodbye. [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/start4life/baby/milestones)


💡 **Learn more:** [read our comprehensive guide](/blog/toddler-language-development-talking)


**Actionable Tip 1:** Encourage your little one's mobility by providing a safe, exploratory environment with age-appropriate toys.

## 💃 13 Months: The Dance of Imitation

By 13 months, your toddler may start imitating your actions and facial expressions – it's adorable, isn't it? They can also understand objects even when hidden and might begin to use simple words like "mama" or "dada." [AAP](https://www.healthychildren.org/English/ages-stages/baby/Pages/Milestones-in-the-First-Year.aspx)


💡 **Learn more:** [read our comprehensive guide](/blog/toddler-social-skills-peers)


**Actionable Tip 2:** Engage in play that involves imitation, such as peekaboo or stacking blocks, to boost your baby's learning and bonding with you.

## 🎨 14 Months: The Art of Exploration

At 14 months, your toddler's curiosity will soar! They'll enjoy exploring their environment, using all five senses to investigate new things. Expect them to become more independent and start feeding themselves with a spoon. [WHO](https://www.who.int/news-room/q-a-detail/early-childhood-development)

**Actionable Tip 3:** Provide safe, stimulating toys for your toddler to explore – this encourages self-discovery and fosters cognitive development.

## 🎭 15 Months: The Show Must Go On

By 15 months, your little one might start showing off their growing repertoire of facial expressions and even begin to pretend play using toys as substitutes for other objects. [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/ncbddd/actearly/milestones/developmental-milestones.html)

**Actionable Tip 4:** Nurture your toddler's pretend play by offering props like toy phones, dolls, or kitchen sets for imaginative play sessions.

## FAQs

1. **My child isn't meeting these milestones – should I be concerned?**
   - Not necessarily! Every child develops at their own pace. However, if you have concerns, reach out to your pediatrician.

2. **How much sleep does my 18-month-old need?**
   - Most 18-month-olds require around 11-14 hours of sleep per day, including naps.

3. **Can I start potty training at 16 months?**
   - While it's possible to start potty training at 16 months, many children are ready between 2 and 3 years old. Consult your pediatrician for advice.

Embrace this incredible journey as you watch your toddler grow and learn! Remember, every step – literally and figuratively – is a testament to their unique growth and development. Happy parenting! 😊🌈
