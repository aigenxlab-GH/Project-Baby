Every expecting parent faces the same overwhelming question: *what do I actually need?* After testing hundreds of products and surveying thousands of real parents, here's our honest guide to baby gear in 2026.

## The True Essentials

These are non-negotiables —  items you genuinely need before baby arrives.

### 1. Infant Car Seat

**Our pick: [Chicco KeyFit 35](/products/car-seats/chicco-keyfit-35-review)**

You cannot leave the hospital without one. The Chicco KeyFit 35 wins for easiest correct installation —  critical for safety.

### 2. Safe Sleep Space

A firm, flat sleep surface in a crib, bassinet, or play yard. The SNOO smart bassinet is the premium choice ($1,600) —  it responds to baby's sounds with motion and white noise. The Babyletto Hudson crib ($479) is our top traditional crib pick.

### 3. Feeding Supplies

- **Breastfeeding**: Nursing bra, breast pads, nipple cream, a quality pump (often covered by insurance)
- **Formula feeding**: Bottles, formula, bottle brush, drying rack
- **Both**: Burp cloths (you need more than you think —  at least 10)

### 4. Diapering Station

A changing pad (no need for a dedicated changing table), diapers, wipes, and diaper cream. That's it.

## What You Can Skip

### Wipe Warmer
Sounds nice. In practice, creates a breeding ground for bacteria and dries out wipes. Skip it.

### Diaper Genie
Any trash can with a lid works. Save the $40.

### Newborn Shoes
Babies don't walk. Shoes are purely decorative until they're cruising furniture (9— 12 months).

### Baby Food Maker
A regular blender does the same thing for $0 more.

### Changing Table
A $30 waterproof changing pad placed on a dresser you already own works identically.

## Final Thoughts

The baby gear industry is designed to make you feel like you need everything. You don't. Start with the true essentials, add smart splurges that match your lifestyle, and ignore the rest. The most important thing you can give your baby costs nothing: time, attention, and love.
