 # Baby Registry Guide: What You Actually Need vs Wants

Welcome to parenthood! Congratulations on your upcoming arrival. Navigating the world of baby registries can be overwhelming, especially when faced with a myriad of items, some necessary and others not so much. Here's a practical guide to help you distinguish between needs and wants, ensuring your little one is well-equipped without breaking the bank.

## Essential Items for Your Baby Registry 👶

According to the [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/pregnancy-and-baby/baby-essentials/), the American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP), and the World Health Organization (WHO), these are must-haves for your baby registry:

1. **Safe Sleeping Space**: A crib, bassinet, or moses basket that meets safety standards is essential for your baby's first few months. [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/safechild/cribs/index.html) provides guidelines to ensure a safe sleep environment.

2. **Clothing**: Newborns grow quickly, so it's best to have a variety of outfits in different sizes. Don't forget hats, socks, and a waterproof bib for those messy feedings!

3. **Diapers and Wipes**: You'll go through countless diapers and wipes in the first few months. Consider eco-friendly options to minimize environmental impact.

4. **Feeding Essentials**: Whether you're breastfeeding or formula feeding, having a breast pump (if applicable), bottles, nipples, and sterilizers will make life easier. [AAP](https://www.healthychildren.org/English/ages-stages/baby/breastfeeding/Pages/Breastfeeding-Equipment.aspx) offers guidance on breastfeeding equipment.

5. **Car Seat**: A rear-facing car seat is crucial for your baby's safety during travel. Make sure it's correctly installed and meets all relevant safety standards. [NHTSA](https://www.nhtsa.gov/equipment/car-seats) provides resources to help you choose the right car seat.

## Nice-to-Have Items for Your Baby Registry 🎁

While not essential, these items can enhance your parenting experience and make life a bit easier:

1. **Diaper Pail**: A diaper pail with an airtight seal helps control odors, making disposal more manageable.

2. **Baby Monitor**: A baby monitor allows you to keep an eye on your little one while they sleep or play in another room. [Consumer Reports](https://www.consumerreports.org/baby-monitors/) offers reviews and recommendations.

3. **Swing or Bouncer**: These can help soothe a fussy baby and give you a much-needed break.

4. **White Noise Machine**: A white noise machine can mimic the sounds your baby heard in the womb, helping them sleep soundly through the night.

## 💭Actionable Tips for Your Baby Registry:

1. **Register at multiple stores:** This gives friends and family a variety of options when choosing gifts.

2. **Create a wish list online:** It's easier for loved ones to find and purchase items from your registry this way.

3. **Request gift cards:** If you already have essentials or prefer certain brands, gift cards can be a thoughtful and useful addition to your registry.

## 🙋‍♀️FAQ Section:

1. **Q**: Should I include diapers in my baby registry?
   **A**: Yes! Diapers are a necessity, and you'll go through them quickly. Register for size newborn and consider eco-friendly options.

2. **Q**: What about gender-neutral gifts for a baby shower?
   **A**: Many items can be used regardless of the baby's gender, such as swings, bouncers, white noise machines, and diaper pails. Focus on essentials and practical gifts.

3. **Q**: How many outfits should I register for newborn size?
   **A**: Newborns grow quickly, so it's best to have around 10-15 newborn-sized outfits on your registry. Don't forget hats, socks, and bibs!
