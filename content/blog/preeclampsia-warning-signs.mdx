 # Preeclampsia Warning Signs Every Pregnant Woman Should Know 🚑🤰

Hello there, future and current mamas! Today, we're diving into a crucial topic that every pregnant woman needs to be aware of: Preeclampsia. This potentially life-threatening condition affects around 5% of pregnancies worldwide (according to the World Health Organization [WHO]). Let's uncover its warning signs and learn how to protect yourself and your baby.

## What is Preeclampsia? 💡

Preeclampsia is a pregnancy complication characterized by high blood pressure and damage to other organs, most often the liver and kidneys (NHS). It usually develops after the 20th week of pregnancy.

## Spotting Preeclampsia Warning Signs 🔍

### High Blood Pressure

Blood pressure is normally below 120/80mmHg. If yours consistently measures above 140/90mmHg, it could be a sign of preeclampsia.

### Protein in Urine (Proteinuria)

Your body filters waste products through your kidneys and into your urine. However, if you have preeclampsia, proteins may leak into your urine. You can check for this at home with a simple urine dipstick test or by visiting your healthcare provider for a urinalysis.

### Swelling 💧

Swelling in the face, hands, and feet is common during pregnancy due to fluid retention. But excessive swelling, especially in the third trimester, could be a symptom of preeclampsia.

### Severe Headaches and Vision Changes 🤕🦯

 persistent headaches or visual disturbances (seeing spots or blurriness) that don't respond to medication can be warning signs.

## Prevention and Actionable Tips 🛑

1. Regular prenatal care: Attend all your scheduled appointments, so your healthcare provider can monitor your blood pressure and check for other symptoms of preeclampsia.
2. Know your numbers: Keep track of your blood pressure readings at home and report any abnormal values to your healthcare provider.
3. Healthy lifestyle: Maintain a balanced diet, rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains; exercise regularly (with your doctor's approval); and avoid smoking and excessive caffeine consumption.

## FAQs 🙋‍♀️

**Q1: Can preeclampsia be detected early?**
A1: Yes! Regular prenatal checkups can help detect preeclampsia early by monitoring blood pressure, urine protein levels, and checking for other symptoms.

**Q2: Is there a cure for preeclampsia?**
A2: No, but the condition can be managed to prevent complications. This often involves delivering the baby prematurely if necessary (under a healthcare provider's supervision).

**Q3: Can preeclampsia affect future pregnancies?**
A3: Yes, women who have had preeclampsia in one pregnancy are at increased risk of developing it again. It's essential to inform your healthcare provider about your history with preeclampsia so they can closely monitor future pregnancies.
