# Best Nursing Pillows 2026: Which Actually Supports Breastfeeding?

Most new parents underestimate how physically demanding breastfeeding is — not just for the baby but for the person doing the feeding. Holding a newborn up to breast height, often eight to twelve times per day, puts sustained load on the forearms, shoulders, upper back, and neck. A nursing pillow does one core job: it brings the baby up to the right height so you stop hunching forward. That single function, done well, can make the difference between feeding that is manageable and feeding that causes ongoing musculoskeletal pain.

This guide covers how to choose the right pillow for your body and feeding style, what distinguishes the top models on the market, which works best after a caesarean section, which holds up for twins, and why not all nursing pillows are created equal.

## Why Feeding Position Matters More Than Most People Think

A poor feeding position contributes to problems beyond sore arms. When a parent leans forward to bring their body to the baby rather than bringing the baby to the breast, the latch tends to suffer too. Baby approaches the nipple from the wrong angle, has to work harder, and may not draw enough breast tissue into the mouth. That can lead to nipple soreness, poor milk transfer, and a frustrated baby.

The correct position — often described as "baby comes to breast, not breast to baby" — requires the baby's body to be at or very close to breast height, with the ear, shoulder, and hip in a straight line. Sustaining that position without props requires holding the baby's entire weight with one arm, repeatedly, for months. A nursing pillow supports the forearm that holds the baby and maintains height without you having to think about it.

Secondary benefits include: giving the winding hand more freedom to pat or rub the baby's back, having a surface to rest a burp cloth on, and providing a gentle surface for short supervised tummy time sessions in the early weeks.

## The Core Things to Look for in a Nursing Pillow

### Shape

Nursing pillows come in three broad shapes.

**C-shape**: The most common. Wraps around your waist with an opening at the front. Easy to put on and take off. The baby lies along the curved surface. Works well for most body types but can slide down and away from the body if the fill is soft.

**U-shape**: Much larger. Wraps around the entire lower torso. Originally designed as a pregnancy support pillow for sleeping on your side, then repositioned for nursing use. Useful if you want one product that works through pregnancy and then into feeding. The Theraline is the best-known example. The main downside is bulk.

**Flat-front / contoured**: My Brest Friend uses a flat front panel rather than a curved one. This presses flush against the abdomen, which prevents the pillow from swinging or sliding away from the body during a feed. This is a significant functional advantage that curved pillows do not offer.

### Firmness

A nursing pillow that squashes flat under the baby's weight has lost its entire function. The pillow needs to be firm enough to hold position under a baby who may weigh anywhere from 3 kg as a newborn to 7 or 8 kg at six months. Squeeze the pillow in the shop or check reviews for comments on fill density. Pillows with a zip cover and accessible fill can be topped up if they flatten over time.

### Washable Cover

This is non-negotiable. Nursing pillows are directly in the way of milk spillage, possetting, and nappy leaks. If the cover is not removable and machine washable, you will be sponge-cleaning a pillow for months. Check the washing temperature the cover tolerates — some covers shrink or warp at high temperatures.

### Portability

A pillow you use on the sofa is different from one you take to a friend's house or a hospital stay. Inflatable travel pillows exist but offer less firm support. The Boppy is relatively compact and easy to carry. My Brest Friend is bulkier.

## My Brest Friend vs Boppy: The Two Most Compared Options

These are the two pillows that almost every breastfeeding guide in the US puts side by side. They are genuinely different products that suit different priorities.

### My Brest Friend Original and Deluxe (around $45–$60 / £40–£55)

The flat front panel is the standout feature. It clips around the body with a buckle at the back and sits firm against your abdomen. The baby cannot slide off the front edge — this is the most common complaint with C-shaped pillows and My Brest Friend solves it by design. The fill is relatively firm. There is a small pocket on the side for a phone or breast pads.

The back support strap adds lumbar support, which is useful for longer feeds. The pillow is quite structured and does not flex into other shapes easily, which is both an advantage (it does its feeding job very well) and a disadvantage (it does not double as a tummy time prop or a seat support in the same way the Boppy does).

For parents whose primary concern is breastfeeding function and position, My Brest Friend is the more purposeful tool.

### Boppy Original Nursing Pillow (around $35–$45 / £30–£40)

The Boppy is softer and more versatile. It does not fasten to the body, which means it can slide away unless you hold it in position, but it also means it is faster to put on and take off. Many parents use the Boppy as a propping support for a baby who is just starting to sit, as a tummy time elevation surface, and as a lounging cushion.

The Boppy's covers are widely available, come in many patterns, and are easily changed. For parents who want one item that supports feeding in the early months and then transitions into general infant positioning support, the Boppy makes sense.

**One important note**: the Boppy Newborn Lounger is a separate product from the Boppy Nursing Pillow. The Boppy Newborn Lounger was voluntarily recalled in the United States in September 2021 following reports of infant deaths from positional asphyxia when babies were left unattended in them. If you use any infant lounger-style product, it must be under continuous supervision only. The Boppy Nursing Pillow itself was not subject to this recall.

### Bobby Pillow (UK)

The Bobby is a UK-market pillow with a similar C-shape to the Boppy. It is widely available in UK supermarkets and pharmacies and sits at a comparable price point. The quality is generally good and the cover is machine washable. It is a reliable mid-range option for UK buyers who do not want to import from the US.

## Theraline Maternity and Nursing Pillow (around £80–£100)

This is the premium versatile option. The Theraline is a large U-shape pillow originally designed to support the abdomen and lower back during pregnancy while sleeping on your side. It can then be repositioned for nursing. The fill is small micro-beads that conform to body shape and can be shifted within the cover.

The advantages are real: it genuinely works for pregnancy sleep support, which is a use case most standard nursing pillows cannot serve at all. The transition to nursing use requires some adjustment. The pillow is large, takes up a significant amount of bed or sofa space, and is expensive compared to purpose-built nursing pillows.

If you plan to buy a pregnancy support pillow anyway, the Theraline is worth the extra cost over buying two separate products. If you are buying specifically for breastfeeding function, it is more money than you need to spend.

## Best Option After a C-Section

After an abdominal surgery, the incision site is a practical problem with nursing. The standard cradle hold requires the baby to rest across the front of the body, which can put pressure on the wound in the first days and weeks. Two approaches help:

**Football hold (underarm hold)**: The baby is tucked along your side, legs going back under your arm, body alongside yours, head at the breast. The baby's body does not cross the incision at all. A firm pillow propped beside you supports the forearm holding the baby.

**Positioning the pillow to protect the wound**: If using a standard cradle hold later in recovery, a pillow held against the abdomen acts as a shield for the scar — the same technique recommended for coughing or sneezing post-surgery.

My Brest Friend tends to work better than the Boppy for C-section recovery because the firm flat panel can be positioned to protect the scar while still supporting the baby.

## Nursing Pillows for Twins

Standard nursing pillows are not wide enough to position two babies simultaneously unless you specifically buy a twin nursing pillow. The My Brest Friend Twin Plus is designed for tandem feeding and is wider than the standard model. The EZ-2-Nurse Twins Pillow is another widely recommended option. Both are significantly larger and heavier than single nursing pillows. Tandem feeding twins is a skill that takes time to establish and is easiest to work through with a lactation consultant or a breastfeeding support group — the pillow helps but it is not the whole solution.

## Can a Regular Pillow Work Instead?

Yes, a firm pillow — folded in half or stacked — can substitute for a nursing pillow. Standard household cushions are usually too soft and squash flat under a baby's weight within seconds, which defeats the purpose. A firm European square pillow, folded once, is the most practical substitute. The main functional gap compared to a dedicated nursing pillow is that a household pillow does not fasten to the body, so it needs to be held in place.

If you are undecided about whether to buy a nursing pillow before birth, one approach is to try with a firm folded pillow in the first few days and assess from there. Many parents find a dedicated nursing pillow genuinely useful; some find folded pillows adequate.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long will I actually use a nursing pillow?**

Most breastfeeding parents use a nursing pillow most actively in the first three to four months, when the baby is heaviest relative to the parent's arm strength and when feeding sessions are longest and most frequent. By five to six months, many parents have developed enough arm strength and body awareness to feed without it for shorter sessions. Many people continue using the pillow for the full duration of breastfeeding, especially for night feeds on a sofa or chair without arms.

**Can I use a nursing pillow for bottle feeding?**

Yes. The positioning function is identical — it brings the baby to the right height to reduce the strain of holding them up for an extended period. This applies to formula feeding and to combination feeding.

**Is My Brest Friend worth the higher price compared to the Boppy?**

For breastfeeding function specifically, most lactation consultants prefer the My Brest Friend because of the flat front panel and the way it prevents the pillow from slipping. If you plan to use it primarily for propping and tummy time rather than feeding, the Boppy's versatility justifies its price. If feeding support is the main use case, My Brest Friend is the more effective tool.

**Is the Boppy Newborn Lounger safe?**

The Boppy Newborn Lounger was recalled in the United States in 2021 following infant deaths linked to positional asphyxia. Any lounger-style product — including alternatives from other brands — should only be used under continuous adult supervision. They are not safe for unsupervised sleep.

**Can I use a nursing pillow in a chair without armrests?**

Yes, and this is one of the main reasons nursing pillows are useful. If you are feeding on a sofa or bed, the pillow substitutes for armrests and allows you to keep the baby at the correct height without relying on chair design.

**My baby keeps sliding off the pillow. What is causing that?**

The most common cause is a pillow that is too soft and has compressed under the baby's weight, creating a slope. The second cause is a C-shape pillow that is not positioned close enough to the body. Try pushing the pillow firmly against your abdomen before placing the baby. If it compresses quickly, the fill has degraded and the pillow needs replacing or re-stuffing. Switching to My Brest Friend, with its flat front that clips to the body, eliminates this problem structurally.

## Key Takeaways

- A nursing pillow's core function is to bring the baby to breast height so you lean forward less — this reduces neck, back, and shoulder strain and supports a better latch.
- My Brest Friend is the more effective feeding tool because its flat front panel prevents slipping and its buckle keeps the pillow in position; the Boppy is softer and more versatile for general infant positioning.
- Firmness matters: a pillow that squashes flat has lost its function. Assess fill density before buying.
- The Boppy Newborn Lounger was recalled in 2021 due to positional asphyxia risk — any lounger-style product must only be used with continuous supervision, never for unsupervised sleep.
- After a C-section, the football hold avoids pressure on the incision; a firm pillow propped beside you supports the forearm and protects the wound.
- For twins, a dedicated twin nursing pillow such as the My Brest Friend Twin Plus is necessary for tandem feeding — standard single pillows are not wide enough.
