 # Third Trimester: Weeks 28-40 Pregnancy Guide


## What to Expect During Weeks 28-40

### H2: Baby Development

Your little one is putting on weight, and their organs are fully formed. They can hear your voice, so feel free to sing lullabies or read bedtime stories.

### H3: Your Body Changes

Your uterus will grow to the size of a watermelon, causing discomfort and shortness of breath. You may also experience Braxton Hicks contractions, which are harmless practice contractions for labor.

### H2: Common Concerns & Solutions

#### H3: Leg Cramps

Leg cramps are common in late pregnancy due to increased pressure on blood vessels and nutrient deficiencies. Staying hydrated, massaging your legs, and stretching regularly can help alleviate discomfort.


💡 **Learn more:** [read our comprehensive guide](/blog/pregnancy-symptoms-by-week)


#### H3: Swelling

Swelling in the feet, ankles, and hands is caused by fluid retention. Elevating your feet, wearing comfortable shoes, and avoiding long periods of standing or sitting can reduce swelling.

## Actionable Tips for a Comfortable Third Trimester

1. **Practice Good Posture:** Maintaining proper posture can help alleviate back pain and improve breathing. Stand tall with your shoulders back and avoid slouching.

2. **Stay Active:** Gentle exercise like walking, swimming, or prenatal yoga can boost mood, reduce stress, and prepare your body for labor. Always consult your healthcare provider before starting a new workout routine.


💡 **Learn more:** [read our comprehensive guide](/blog/when-to-go-to-hospital-labor)


3. **Prioritize Rest:** Make sure to get plenty of rest and listen to your body when it needs a break. This will help you conserve energy for the final stretch and your upcoming delivery.

## FAQs

### Q: Is it safe to travel during the third trimester?

A: It's generally safe to travel in the third trimester, but consult with your healthcare provider beforehand. Long car rides or flights can increase the risk of blood clots, so plan breaks along the way and wear compression stockings if needed.

### Q: How do I know when it's time to go to the hospital?

A: Call your healthcare provider immediately if you experience vaginal bleeding, persistent contractions, or a decrease in fetal movement. You should also head to the hospital if your water breaks or you have severe abdominal pain.

### Q: Can I still sleep on my back in the third trimester?

A: It's best to avoid sleeping on your back during the third trimester, as it can put pressure on your organs and blood vessels. Instead, try sleeping on your side with a pillow between your legs for added comfort.

As you move closer to your due date, remember to relax and enjoy this special time. Your baby is almost here, and soon you'll be embarking on the incredible journey of parenthood!
