Baby carriers transform early parenthood. The ability to have your baby close while keeping your hands free — for housework, walks, older children, or simply sitting down with a coffee — is a genuine quality-of-life upgrade.

The Ergobaby Omni 360 has been our top structured carrier pick for several years. Here's why.

## What Makes the Omni 360 Different

Most structured carriers require a separate newborn insert (an extra $40–50 purchase) before you can use them from birth. The Omni 360 has a built-in newborn position — the seat simply adjusts to a narrower width that safely supports newborns from 3.2 kg.

The other differentiator is the four carry positions — including front-facing out, which many parents specifically want but which is absent from many carriers (it requires specific design to maintain the ergonomic M-position).

## Comfort: The Wearer Experience

The padded waistband with lumbar support is one of the best in the category. At 2+ hours of wearing with a heavier baby, the weight distribution feels genuinely balanced — hips and shoulders share the load rather than everything pulling forward on your back.

The shoulder straps are wide and padded, and the chest clip adjusts easily while wearing.

The only consistent complaint is warmth in summer — the cotton construction and snug fit mean both parent and baby get hot. The Omni 360 Cool Air version addresses this with mesh panelling.

## Comfort: Baby's Experience

The M-position (knees higher than bottom) is certified by the International Hip Dysplasia Institute. This position supports healthy hip development — critical in the first months when hip joints are still forming.

All four carry positions maintain this ergonomic position.

## Learning Curve

The Omni 360 has more adjustments than simpler carriers, and it takes most new users several tries to feel confident. Watch a video tutorial specifically for the position you're using — Ergobaby's official YouTube channel has excellent guides.

Once it clicks, adjustments become second nature.

## Our Verdict

At $209, the Omni 360 is an investment that replaces what would otherwise be 2–3 different carriers across your baby's first years. Newborn ready, toddler capable, four positions, machine washable, and genuinely comfortable for extended wear.
