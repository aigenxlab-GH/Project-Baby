**The Tommee Tippee First Sips Trainer Cup is a budget-friendly sippy cup designed for families already using Tommee Tippee bottles. Its soft silicone spout and familiar transition make it ideal for parents prioritizing brand consistency, while the 360° grip helps little hands learn to drink independently. At $9, it’s an affordable option with BPA-free materials and dishwasher safety.**

## Design & Build  
The Tommee Tippee First Sips Trainer Cup combines simplicity with functional design. Its 7-ounce capacity is standard for a beginner’s sippy cup, making it suitable for 6-month-olds and up. The cup is constructed from durable Tritan plastic (body) and features a soft silicone spout, a material choice that aligns with the brand’s bottle nipples to ease sensory transitions for babies. The 360° color-band grip is ergonomically designed to fit small hands, with a textured surface that prevents slipping during messy meals. The BPA-free certification is a critical safety checkmark, especially for parents monitoring chemical exposure. While the handle isn’t as wide as some competitors (e.g., Munchkin 360), its narrow band is lightweight and doesn’t add bulk. The spill-proof valve is basic but effective for preventing leaks in a cup designed for beginners. For parents who prioritize tactile familiarity, the spout’s texture and flexibility mirror Tommee Tippee’s bottle nipples, reducing the likelihood of rejection during the transition to open cups. The overall design is minimalist, with no unnecessary accessories or gimmicks, which contributes to its affordability.  

## Performance  
The First Sips cup performs reliably as a transitional sippy cup for infants learning to drink independently. The soft silicone spout is gentle on teeth and gums, similar to how Tommee Tippee bottle nipples are engineered for comfort. For families already using the brand’s bottles, this consistency can ease the shift to open cups, as babies recognize the familiar texture. The 360° grip band is a standout feature for small hands, allowing babies to grasp the cup securely even during wobbly attempts to drink. However, the spout design—a traditional open mouth with a valve—is less advanced than valveless straws or anti-colic systems found in higher-end models. This means it doesn’t fully address common concerns like tooth decay from prolonged sipping or spillage during movement. The cup’s 7-ounce capacity is sufficient for short sipping sessions but may require frequent refilling during long outings. Dishwasher safety is a practical advantage for cleaning, though the narrow opening can make handwashing slightly tedious. While it doesn’t offer the anti-tilt stability of Contigo’s Smart Lid system, it’s sturdy enough for toddlers who aren’t yet experts at holding cups. For parents prioritizing brand continuity over cutting-edge features, this cup is a dependable choice.  

## What We Like  
The First Sips cup shines in three key areas. First, for families already invested in the Tommee Tippee ecosystem, the seamless transition is invaluable. A parent using Tommee Tippee bottles might find their 8-month-old resists a new brand’s cup due to texture differences, but the First Sips spout’s familiar softness encourages acceptance. Second, the 360° color-band grip is a thoughtful design for toddlers learning to grasp. During mealtime, a 9-month-old with developing motor skills can confidently hold the cup without slipping, reducing frustration for both baby and parent. Third, the BPA-free materials and affordability make it a low-risk option for trial and error. For parents hesitant to commit to a pricier model (like Munchkin’s 360), this cup offers a cost-effective way to test a child’s preference for a soft spout over a hard one. Real-world scenarios, like a busy morning routine where a toddler grips the cup independently or a parent sterilizing it with their Tommee Tippee bottles, highlight its practicality. While it lacks premium features, the cup excels as a straightforward, budget-conscious solution for brand-aligned households.  

## Drawbacks  
The First Sips cup’s simplicity is also its limitation. The traditional spout design, while familiar to Tommee Tippee users, shares the same dental concerns as other open-sip cups: prolonged contact with liquids can increase the risk of tooth decay. Parents who prioritize oral health might prefer alternatives with closed-valve systems or straw designs that encourage shorter sipping bursts. Additionally, the cup is less feature-rich than competitors like the Munchkin 360 or Contigo Sippy Cup. For instance, the absence of a valve or anti-tilt mechanism means minor spills are more likely during active play or if the cup is dropped. The UK-based brand’s distribution is another hurdle: while the cup is widely available in the U.S. via major retailers, parents in remote areas may face inconsistent stock or higher shipping costs. Finally, the design lacks innovation for those seeking advanced training tools. A parent who wants to transition their child to an open cup might find this model insufficient compared to a Munchkin 360’s dual-sided design, which combines a valve and a training lip to ease the shift.  

## Buying Guide  
When choosing a sippy cup, consider your child’s developmental stage and your priorities. For infants 6–12 months, a soft spout with a 6–8 oz capacity is ideal for short sipping sessions. Look for BPA-free and lead-free materials, and avoid cups with hard plastic or metal components that could damage teeth. Dishwasher-safe construction is a must for busy parents, and a secure lid is essential to prevent leaks in bags or diaper pouches.  

A common mistake is delaying the introduction of a sippy cup until 12 months; starting earlier helps babies practice oral motor skills and prepares them for open cups. Avoid cups with long, narrow straws for very young toddlers, as they can be difficult to sip from. Similarly, choose models with wide mouths for easy cleaning and refilling.  

For brand consistency, stick to a system (like Tommee Tippee) if your baby is already accustomed to its bottle nipples. However, if you’re new to sippy cups, consider models with innovative features like anti-tilt valves (Contigo) or dual-sided designs (Munchkin). Always test different spout types—soft, hard, or straw—to see what your child prefers. Lastly, prioritize durability: a cup that withstands daily drops and bites will last longer, saving money in the long run.
