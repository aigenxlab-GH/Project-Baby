**The **THERMOS FOOGO** Leak-Proof Stainless Steel Sippy Cup is a premium option for parents of 6–12 month olds seeking a durable, dental-friendly hydration solution. With Thermos’s signature insulation heritage and a 7 oz size ideal for infants, this cup keeps drinks cold for 8 hours and features a soft silicone straw to protect emerging teeth. While its stainless steel build and leak-proof design make it a standout for on-the-go use, the higher price and weight require consideration.**

## ## Design & Build  

The Thermos Foogo’s 7 oz stainless steel body is vacuum-insulated, offering a sleek, modern aesthetic with a matte finish that resists fingerprints. Weighing 12.8 oz (363g), the cup feels sturdy but may feel heavy for younger infants still developing grip strength. The soft silicone straw is integrated into a leak-proof valve system, with a textured exterior for better handling. Unlike typical plastic sippy cups, the Foogo avoids leaching chemicals, thanks to its stainless steel construction. The straw is BPA-free and designed to compress slightly under pressure, making it gentler on teething gums. The cup’s narrow profile allows it to fit in most standard cup holders, though its weight might require a secure holder to prevent tipping. For parents prioritizing durability and safety, the Foogo’s design balances functionality with Thermos’s century-old insulation expertise.  

The vacuum insulation technology used in the Foogo is similar to that found in Thermos’s classic thermos flasks, ensuring that beverages stay cold for up to 8 hours. This feature is particularly beneficial for parents preparing chilled milk or water for car rides or park visits. The insulation also prevents condensation, keeping surfaces dry and reducing the risk of slippery accidents. The cup’s exterior is textured to aid in a baby’s grip, but its weight can be a challenge for infants under 9 months who lack the strength to hold it upright independently.  

## ## Performance  

The Foogo excels in maintaining drink temperatures, leveraging Thermos’s vacuum insulation to keep beverages cold for up to 8 hours—ideal for outdoor outings or car trips. Whether filled with chilled water, milk, or juice, the cup resists condensation, preventing moisture buildup on surfaces. The soft silicone straw delivers liquid behind the front teeth, reducing the risk of "sippy cup tooth decay" caused by prolonged exposure to sugary liquids. This design also mimics adult drinking mechanics, encouraging proper tongue positioning for swallowing. The leak-proof valve works reliably when sealed, though occasional leakage has been reported if the cup is shaken vigorously or tipped beyond 90 degrees. The 7 oz capacity is appropriate for infants still transitioning from bottles, avoiding overfilling that can lead to spills or refusal to drink. Parents note that the straw’s flexibility allows babies to sip comfortably, even during teething discomfort, though the valve’s tight seal may require practice for younger users to open easily.  

In real-world use, the Foogo’s 8-hour cold retention is a game-changer for parents with active lifestyles. For example, filling the cup with ice-cold water before a morning walk or a trip to the grocery store ensures the drink remains refreshingly cool, even on sunny days. The leak-proof seal has been tested in scenarios like tossing the cup into a diaper bag or placing it in a car cup holder—no leaks or drips. However, the valve’s tightness can be a double-edged sword: while it prevents spills, it may confuse babies who haven’t yet learned to push the valve open with their lips. Parents often need to demonstrate the motion initially, but most infants adapt within a few days.

## ## Drawbacks  

1. **Higher Price Point**: At $22, the Foogo is significantly more expensive than plastic alternatives (e.g., Munchkin or Playtex sippy cups priced at $7–$12). While the price reflects durability, budget-conscious parents may find it cost-prohibitive for short-term use.

## ## Buying Guide  

When choosing a sippy cup, consider these factors:  
- **Age Appropriateness**: For 6–12 month olds, 7 oz cups with soft straws or spouts are ideal. Larger cups (12–16 oz) suit toddlers who are transitioning to open cups.  
- **Material Safety**: Prioritize stainless steel or BPA-free plastic to avoid chemical leaching. Avoid cups with hard plastic valves that can damage teething gums.  
- **Dental Health**: Opt for straws or spouts that position liquid delivery behind the front teeth. The American Academy of Pediatrics warns that front-facing spouts increase decay risk.  
- **Ease of Cleaning**: Cups with removable straws or spouts require dedicated brushes. For busy parents, choose designs that allow quick disassembly.  
- **Common Mistakes**: Overfilling cups can lead to spills; stick to the 7 oz range for infants. Avoid letting babies sleep with cups containing sugary drinks, even in straws.  

## ## Safe Use & Setup  

Setting up the Thermos Foogo sippy cup requires a few key steps to ensure safety and functionality. First, disassemble all components (straw, valve, and cup) and rinse them under hot water. Using a dedicated bottle brush with a soft bristle, scrub the inside of the cup and the narrow straw to remove any manufacturing residue. For added safety, sterilize the cup in boiling water or a dishwasher-safe cycle for 10 minutes before first use.  

To test the leak-proof seal, fill the cup halfway with water, close the valve, and shake it vigorously. No leaks should occur if the valve is properly sealed. For daily use, always close the valve when not in use to prevent spills. When cleaning, use a silicone-safe dish soap, as harsh chemicals can degrade the straw over time. Store the cup in a dry, upright position to avoid bacterial growth.  

Safety tips include avoiding hot liquids (the cup is rated for cold beverages only) and inspecting the straw and valve weekly for wear, such as cracks or deformities. If the valve becomes loose, replace the cup immediately to prevent leaks. The weight of the cup (12.8 oz) should not be used as a toy to avoid choking hazards for infants under 12 months.

The Foogo’s vacuum insulation and stainless steel construction give it a durability edge, but its higher price and cleaning complexity may deter some parents. For families prioritizing insulation and dental health, the Foogo is unmatched. For those seeking affordability or simplicity, the Munchkin or Nuk may be better starting points.
