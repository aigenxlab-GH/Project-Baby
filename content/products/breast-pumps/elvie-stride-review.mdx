The **Elvie Stride** represents what wearable technology can accomplish when applied to a genuine daily challenge. For mothers who pump 2–3 times per workday and spend 20–30 minutes per session connected to a traditional pump, the Elvie Stride reclaims those hours.

## Hands-Free Means Actually Hands-Free

Traditional double electric pumps require you to hold the flanges against your body (or wear a pumping bra), connect to a motor via tubes, and sit near an outlet. The Elvie Stride fits inside a nursing bra with no external tubes. The motor, collection cups, and battery are all contained in each pod. You can stand, walk, type, sit in meetings, or drive while pumping.

This isn't a marginal convenience improvement — it fundamentally changes what you can do during pumping sessions.

## App Connectivity

The Elvie app (iOS and Android) connects via Bluetooth to track volume pumped (via sensors in each pod), session duration, and pumping history. You can adjust speed and suction mode from your phone without reaching into your shirt. The app provides insights over time to help optimize sessions.

## Quiet Operation

At under 48 dB, the Elvie Stride is quieter than traditional pumps. Not silent — in a completely quiet office someone nearby might hear a soft hum — but quiet enough for most professional settings.

## The Trade-offs

At $380, the Elvie Stride is a premium product. Some users report lower milk output compared to hospital-grade pumps — partly a fit issue (flanges must be correctly sized) and partly because wearable pump mechanics differ from traditional pumps. For maximum supply maintenance, many lactation consultants suggest pairing the Elvie with a traditional pump.
