The **Spectra S1 Plus** has become the most recommended breast pump by lactation consultants in the US — and after extensive testing against competitors at every price point, our experience confirms why it earned that reputation.

## Hospital-Grade Performance in a Consumer Package

"Hospital-grade" typically means suction strength of 250+ mmHg and cycle speeds that efficiently stimulate letdown. The Spectra S1 achieves 300 mmHg — matching hospital rental units that cost $100+/month. This matters for supply: strong, efficient pumping sessions stimulate and maintain milk production better than weaker consumer pumps.

## The Battery Advantage

The S2 (which lacks battery) is the most common insurance-covered pump. The S1 adds a rechargeable battery that lasts approximately 3 hours of continuous pumping — enough for a full workday of pumping sessions between charges. This enables pumping in cars, on planes, in conference rooms, or anywhere an outlet isn't convenient.

## Quiet Operation

At under 45 dB, the Spectra is noticeably quieter than older or cheaper pumps. You can pump in an office with a conversation happening nearby without it being obvious.

## The Closed System

The backflow protector is a physical barrier that prevents milk from entering the motor or tubing. This makes the pump more hygienic (important for immune-compromised mothers), extends pump life, and means the tubing doesn't need to be replaced as frequently.


## Who It's For

The **Spectra S1 Plus Electric Breast Pump** is the right choice when choosing a breast pump if:
- You want quality features without paying for top-tier branding
- Rechargeable battery — pump anywhere without a power outlet is a priority for you
- Hospital-grade suction (up to 300 mmHg) matters in your household
- The spec that matters most for your situation: **Type: Double electric**

**Think twice if:** Bulkier and heavier than wearable pumps.

## Safety Standards & Certifications

Breast pumps classified as milk-expression devices are regulated by the **FDA as Class II medical devices**, requiring 510(k) clearance. All parts that contact milk must be **BPA-free** and phthalate-free. Under the Affordable Care Act, most insurance plans are required to cover a breast pump — check your insurer before purchasing.

## Parent Tips

- Before buying, account for this known limitation: **Bulkier and heavier than wearable pumps**. Understand whether this affects your specific situation.
- Before buying, account for this known limitation: **Closed system requires specific Spectra flanges**. Understand whether this affects your specific situation.
- Flange size matters more than any other pump component — measure nipple diameter and size up by 2–3mm for the correct fit.
- Clean pump parts in hot soapy water after every session — dishwasher cleaning degrades silicone parts faster.
