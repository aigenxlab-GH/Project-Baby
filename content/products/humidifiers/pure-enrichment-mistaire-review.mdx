**The Pure Enrichment MistAire Ultrasonic Cool Mist Humidifier is a budget-friendly, compact solution for parents with small nurseries or tight spaces. Designed for infants and toddlers, this humidifier combines 16 hours of continuous runtime with a discreet, easy-to-place design. While its smaller coverage area and blue nightlight may not suit every home, its affordability and space-saving footprint make it a standout choice for very small rooms under 150 square feet.**

## Design & Build  
The MistAire’s build is defined by its compact, minimalist profile. At 6.5 inches tall and 5 inches wide, it’s one of the smallest humidifiers on the market, making it ideal for cramped nurseries, nightstands, or floating shelves. Constructed from durable, BPA-free plastic, the unit feels lightweight yet sturdy in hand, with a smooth matte finish that resists fingerprints. The base is slightly weighted to prevent tipping, though it lacks the premium feel of higher-end models. The 1.5L water tank is transparent, allowing users to monitor water levels at a glance, but the narrow fill opening (1.25 inches in diameter) makes refilling a bit cumbersome.  

Aesthetically, the MistAire is unassuming—no flashy details or colorful accents—but its neutral white design blends into most nursery decors. The blue LED nightlight, while functional, lacks the warm, sleep-friendly glow of amber options found in competitors like the FridaBaby Humidifier. For parents prioritizing design, the MistAire’s understated look is a neutral pro or con depending on personal preference. Compared to bulkier models like the Crane Drop (8.5" x 6.5" x 10.5") or Levoit LV600HH (8.6" x 5.9" x 11.8"), the MistAire’s footprint is significantly smaller, making it a better fit for apartments or homes with limited space. However, its plastic construction feels less durable than the metal or glass bases of pricier models, which may raise concerns about longevity in high-use environments.

## Performance  
The MistAire delivers reliable humidity control for its intended use case: small, targeted spaces. The 1.5L tank provides 16 hours of continuous cool mist, which is sufficient for overnight use in a 12x13-foot nursery without needing a refill. The ultrasonic technology operates quietly, emitting a soft hum rather than the louder mechanical whir of some evaporative models. The 360-degree adjustable nozzle is a standout feature, allowing parents to direct mist precisely toward cribs or bassinets, maximizing humidity at the baby’s sleeping area rather than dispersing it throughout the room. This precision reduces humidity loss in drafty or larger spaces.  

In real-world testing, the MistAire maintains a consistent output even in low-humidity environments, such as winter months or high-altitude regions. However, the coverage area of 160 sq ft means it struggles to maintain consistent humidity in open-plan rooms or larger nurseries. For optimal performance, place it in a corner close to the baby’s crib to minimize distance and airflow interference. While the unit lacks a humidistat or auto shut-off when the tank is empty, the large water window and visible mist output make manual monitoring straightforward. Users with infants prone to colds or allergies may appreciate the cool mist’s ability to soothe dry airways, but those with asthma should consult a doctor before use, as excess humidity can promote mold growth.

## What We Like  
1. **Space-Saving Design for Small Nurseries**: The MistAire’s compact size is a practical win for parents with limited shelf or counter space. Unlike bulkier models like the Crane Drop, it can nestle into tight corners or sit atop a narrow shelf, freeing up room for other essentials like baby monitors or nightlights. Its lightweight design also makes it easy to move between rooms during the day.  
2. **16-Hour Overnight Runtime**: The 16-hour runtime from a single 1.5L tank ensures uninterrupted humidity during critical sleep hours. Parents can fill the tank before bedtime and avoid mid-night refills, a significant advantage over shorter-running humidifiers like the Vicks Warm Mist Humidifier, which typically requires refills every 10–12 hours.  
3. **Targeted Mist Direction**: The 360-degree mist nozzle allows precise control over where the humidity is delivered. In practice, this means parents can aim the mist directly at a baby’s crib, avoiding unnecessary humidity in high-traffic areas of the room. This feature is particularly useful in rooms with fluctuating temperatures or air currents from windows or vents.  

## Drawbacks  
1. **Limited Coverage for Larger Spaces**: The 160 sq ft coverage is adequate for a standard small nursery but falls short for larger rooms or open layouts. Parents in bigger spaces may find the MistAire insufficient, requiring supplemental humidifiers or relocation of the unit. The Crane Drop, which covers 500 sq ft, is a better choice for multi-room use.  
2. **Narrow, Difficult-to-Fill Tank Opening**: The tank’s small opening (1.25 inches) makes filling a fussy process, especially with a bottle spout. Overfilling or spills are common during refills, and the lack of a wide mouth increases the risk of water splashing onto the base, potentially causing condensation issues. A funnel or a water jug with a narrow spout is recommended.  
3. **Blue Nightlight Less Ideal for Sleep**: While the optional blue LED nightlight is soft and ambient, studies suggest blue light may disrupt circadian rhythms more than warmer amber tones. The FridaBaby Humidifier and Crane Drop offer sleep-friendly amber options, making them better choices for parents seeking light therapy for nighttime feedings.  

## Safe Use & Setup  
Proper setup and safety precautions are essential for the MistAire’s effective and safe operation. Begin by placing the humidifier on a flat, stable surface at least 3 feet away from electrical outlets, cribs, or baby monitors to prevent accidental tipping or electrical hazards. Fill the tank with distilled water using a funnel to avoid spills, and never exceed the maximum fill line (marked inside the tank). The narrow opening may require patience; using a jug with a narrow spout helps.  

Before turning it on, ensure the unit is fully assembled and the base is dry. The nightlight can be toggled on or off via the side button, but for infants under 6 months, consider turning it off to avoid light exposure during sleep. Clean the tank weekly with white vinegar or a mild detergent to prevent bacterial growth, and descale it monthly using a commercial descaling solution. Never operate the unit when it’s empty, as this can damage the ultrasonic motor. For households with hard water, use a water softener to reduce mineral buildup in the tank. Always keep the humidifier out of reach of children once it’s running, and avoid placing it near heat sources like radiators or direct sunlight.  

## How It Compares  
The MistAire is best compared to other compact humidifiers in its price range, such as the Levoit LV120HH and Crane Drop. While the MistAire excels in size and affordability ($40 vs. $70 for the Levoit), it lags in coverage and features. The Levoit offers a 400 sq ft coverage and a humidistat, making it more versatile for growing families. The Crane Drop, priced at $130, covers 500 sq ft and includes auto shut-off and a humidistat, but its larger size makes it unwieldy for small spaces. The MistAire’s 16-hour runtime outperforms the Levoit’s 20-hour runtime in real-world use due to its lower output, which is ideal for overnight use in small rooms. For parents prioritizing sleep-friendly lighting, the FridaBaby Humidifier ($130) offers an amber nightlight and a built-in monitor, though its $200 price tag is steep for a secondary unit. Overall, the MistAire is the best choice for budget-conscious parents with small nurseries, while the Levoit or Crane Drop suits those needing broader coverage or advanced features.

## Verdict  
With an 8.3/10 score, the Pure Enrichment MistAire is a solid choice for parents with very small nurseries or compact living spaces. Its 16-hour runtime, adjustable mist nozzle, and budget-friendly price make it ideal for targeted humidity control in rooms under 150 sq ft. However, its limited coverage, narrow fill opening, and blue nightlight may deter those with larger spaces or specific lighting needs. If you’re upgrading from a basic humidifier or need a secondary unit for a guest room, the MistAire excels. For primary nursery use in standard-sized rooms, consider the Crane Drop or Levoit LV600HH for broader coverage and more robust features.
