The **Fisher-Price Deluxe Bouncer: Calm Vibes** is one of the best-selling baby bouncers for good reason: it's simple, affordable, and genuinely useful for the first four months of life.

## What Is It and How Does It Work

This is a passive bouncer — meaning it doesn't swing or rock automatically. Instead, it responds to your baby's natural movements, bouncing gently as they wiggle. The built-in vibration (powered by 2 D batteries) adds a low hum that many babies find calming, similar to riding in a car.

## Best For: Hands-Free Moments

The Calm Vibes bouncer shines when you need two hands but want to keep baby close. Cook dinner, fold laundry, shower — baby can sit safely nearby in a semi-reclined position with full visibility of you.

## Portability

At under 5 lbs, this bouncer is genuinely portable. The flat fold means it fits in the trunk of any car and moves easily between rooms. Many families buy two — one for upstairs and one for downstairs.

## Limitations

Once your baby can sit up unassisted (typically around 4 months), they've outgrown this bouncer. It's truly a newborn-to-early-infant product. Also note: there's no motorized rocking, which can be a dealbreaker for parents of particularly fussy babies who need more active movement.

## Our Verdict

At $60, the Fisher-Price Deluxe Bouncer is practically a no-brainer as a secondary soothing tool. It won't replace a full swing for fussy babies, but it earns its place in any nursery setup.
