The **Ingenuity ConvertMe Swing-2-Seat** addresses a real parent problem: what do you do when your baby naps in the swing but you need to move to another room? The answer: bring the seat with you.

## The Conversion Feature

The swing's seat detaches from its frame to become a standalone infant seat. It takes about 30 seconds, no tools required. The detached seat has a handle for carrying and rocks gently on its curved base. It's not a replacement for a dedicated bouncer, but it's genuinely useful for moving a sleeping baby.

## As a Full Swing

In swing mode, the ConvertMe performs solidly. Six speeds, a 16-melody library, nature sounds, and a Bluetooth speaker cover most soothing scenarios. The timer function gradually slows the swing — useful if you want baby to transition to unassisted sleep.

## What We Found in Testing

Most babies we observed settled well in the swing. The seat is well-padded and the 5-point harness is easy to buckle. The Bluetooth speaker streams from your phone, allowing you to play specific sounds your baby responds to — a big advantage over fixed-sound machines.

## The Trade-offs

The conversion feature adds bulk to the frame. It's wider than the Fisher-Price bouncer or Graco Glider. The portable seat mode also rocks less smoothly than dedicated bouncers like the BabyBjörn Balance Soft.

## Bottom Line

If you want a swing and occasionally need to move a sleeping baby, the ConvertMe is a clever solution. If you want the absolute best swing OR the absolute best bouncer, buy them separately.

## Safety Standards & Certifications

Infant swings must meet **ASTM F2088**, which covers structural strength, restraint integrity, and stability across swing arc. The **CPSC** prohibits marketing swings as sleep products unless they meet flat-sleep safety standards. A baby who falls asleep in a swing should be transferred to a firm, flat sleep surface.

The **Ingenuity ConvertMe Swing-2-Seat** scores 8/10 — safety standards compliance is factored into this rating and was verified against current CPSC and ASTM requirements at time of review.
