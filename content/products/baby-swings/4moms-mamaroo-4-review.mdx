The **4moms mamaRoo 4** isn't just a baby swing — it's a smart soothing system that's won over sleep-deprived parents worldwide. We tested it for three months with a newborn, and here's everything you need to know.

## What Makes the mamaRoo Different

Traditional baby swings move back-and-forth or side-to-side. The mamaRoo offers 5 distinct motions:
- **Car ride** — gentle bouncing with side-to-side motion
- **Kangaroo** — up-and-down bounce
- **Tree swing** — side-to-side arc
- **Rock-a-bye** — traditional rocking
- **Wave** — figure-eight movement

Each of these mimics motions parents naturally use to soothe a baby, and you can pair any with 5 speed settings and built-in sounds.

## App Control Is a Game Changer

The free 4moms app (iOS and Android) lets you change speed, motion, and sound without disturbing a sleeping baby. Set a timer to gradually slow down as your baby drifts off. It's a small feature that makes a big difference at 3am.

## Design and Footprint

The mamaRoo's upright seat and single-post design use far less floor space than traditional arc swings. It fits next to a couch without blocking a room. The seat fabric is machine washable — a must.

## What We'd Change

The seat only reclines to about 45 degrees — more vertical than some parents expect. Very young newborns may need the separate infant insert for proper head support. And at ~$280, it's a significant investment for something your baby uses for only a few months.

## Who Should Buy It

The mamaRoo 4 is ideal for parents with fussy or colicky babies who haven't responded to traditional swings, and for parents in smaller spaces who need a compact footprint. If your baby is easily soothed, a $100 swing will do just as well.

## Safety Standards & Certifications

Infant swings must meet **ASTM F2088**, which covers structural strength, restraint integrity, and stability across swing arc. The **CPSC** prohibits marketing swings as sleep products unless they meet flat-sleep safety standards. A baby who falls asleep in a swing should be transferred to a firm, flat sleep surface.
