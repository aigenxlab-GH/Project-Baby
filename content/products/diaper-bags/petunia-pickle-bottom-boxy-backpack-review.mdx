## Safe Use & Setup  
To ensure the **Petunia Pickle Bottom Boxy Backpack** is used safely and effectively, follow these setup and usage tips:  
1. **Adjust Straps for Ergonomic Support**: The padded shoulder straps should be adjusted to fit your body comfortably, distributing weight evenly between both shoulders. Avoid overloading the bag, which can strain your back or shoulders. For hands-free use, clip the bag securely to a stroller using the provided clips, ensuring it doesn’t dangle or swing freely.  
2. **Secure the Changing Pad**: The built-in changing pad is a great feature, but always check that it’s properly inserted into the designated compartment. Secure it with the zip to prevent it from shifting during movement. When using the pad in public restrooms, place the bag on a clean surface to avoid contamination.  
3. **Avoid Overstuffing**: While the Boxy Backpack is spacious, overloading it can compromise its structure and make it difficult to access items. Prioritize essentials like diapers, wipes, a change of clothes, and a small toy. Keep electronics and heavy items in a separate compartment to maintain balance.  
4. **Child Safety Tips**: When placing the bag on a stroller or bench, ensure it’s stable and won’t tip over if a curious baby reaches for it. Avoid leaving the bag unattended in high-traffic areas to prevent tripping hazards. For newborns, never leave the baby unattended near the bag, especially if it’s placed on a soft surface.

## Buying Guide  
When selecting a **diaper bag**, consider your **lifestyle** and **parenting priorities**.  
- **Age Range**: Diaper bags are essential from 0–3 years, but many parents continue using them for day-trippers until kids are potty-trained. Look for bags with **insulated bottle pockets** for older infants and **snack compartments** for toddlers.  
- **Safety Tips**: Ensure the bag has a **secure changing pad** (look for non-slip lining) and **adjustable straps** to prevent strain. Avoid bags with sharp corners or flimsy materials that could snag on stroller frames.  
- **Common Mistakes**: Overloading is a frequent issue—stick to essential items like diapers, wipes, a change of clothes, and a small toy to prevent the bag from becoming a “black hole.” Also, avoid buying based solely on aesthetics; prioritize functionality for your daily routine.  
- **Style vs. Function**: For multi-taskers, balance looks with practicality. Structured bags like the Boxy Backpack suit fashion-first parents, while convertible totes are better for ultra-organized individuals. Always test the bag in person to assess comfort and compartment layout.
