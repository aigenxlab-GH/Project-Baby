The **UPPAbaby VISTA V2** has been the go-to stroller for design-conscious parents who want a premium, versatile ride from birth through toddlerhood. After testing it across city sidewalks, gravel paths, and mall floors, here's our honest take.

## Who Is the VISTA V2 For?

The VISTA V2 is ideal for parents who:
- Plan to have more than one child (it converts to a double)
- Want a bassinet included for a newborn
- Live in urban areas and need smooth sidewalk performance
- Value build quality and are willing to pay for it

## Design & Build Quality

From the moment you unfold the VISTA V2, you can feel the premium construction. The frame is aluminium with a satin finish, and every joint feels solid. The leatherette handlebar is comfortable for tall and short parents alike —  it adjusts from 40.5" to 44".

## Maneuverability

This is where the VISTA V2 shines brightest. The swivel front wheels glide smoothly on city pavement, and locking them for rough terrain takes one quick click. We navigated it through a busy farmers' market, a gravel park path, and a shopping mall without any issue.

## The Bassinet

The included VISTA bassinet is **certified safe for overnight sleep** —  a feature many competitor strollers charge extra for. It's roomy, well-ventilated, and attaches to the frame in seconds.

## Storage

The under-seat storage basket is enormous —  genuinely the best we've tested. It holds a large diaper bag, groceries, and a blanket simultaneously, and is easily accessible from any angle.

## The Downsides

At 26 lbs without accessories, the VISTA V2 is not a light stroller. Lifting it into a car trunk by yourself takes some effort. The fold, while one-handed, results in a bulky package. If you have a small car or live in a walkup apartment, factor this in.

## Our Verdict

The UPPAbaby VISTA V2 earns its premium price through sheer versatility, excellent build quality, and the included bassinet. For families who plan to grow, it pays for itself. For single-child families, consider the lighter UPPAbaby CRUZ V2 instead.
