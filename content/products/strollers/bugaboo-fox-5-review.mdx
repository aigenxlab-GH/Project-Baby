The **Bugaboo Fox 5** is the pushchair equivalent of a luxury saloon car: you notice the quality the moment you grip the handlebar.

## The Ride

Bugaboo's four-wheel suspension system is the best we've tested. Where other pushchairs judder over kerb edges and cobblestones, the Fox 5 absorbs them. After three months of city use, our test baby slept through road surfaces that woke him in every other pushchair we tested.

## The Fold

The self-standing fold is one of those features you don't appreciate until you have it. Click the fold mechanism, lower the stroller, and it stands upright on its rear wheels and frame. No leaning it against the car. No laying it in the mud.

## Reversible Seat

Switching from parent-facing to world-facing takes about 5 seconds. Two buttons, lift, click. Once you've had a reversible seat, it's hard to go back — being able to talk to your baby or monitor them effortlessly is genuinely useful.

## The Bassinet Situation

At $1,399, not including a bassinet is a frustration. Competitors at half the price include one. Budget an additional $299–399 for the Bugaboo Fox 5 Bassinet if you're buying this for a newborn.

## Our Verdict

The Fox 5 is the best full-size pushchair on the market. For single-child families who want the finest daily experience and have the budget, it's the one to get. Families planning two children should consider the UPPAbaby Vista V2's double-configuration advantage.

## Safety Standards & Certifications

Strollers must meet **ASTM F833**, which covers stability, braking, structural integrity, and restraint systems. JPMA-certified strollers carry an independent quality verification seal. Always use the provided harness — stroller injuries are most commonly caused by falls from an unrestrained child.

The **Bugaboo Fox 5** scores 9.2/10 — safety standards compliance is factored into this rating and was verified against current CPSC and ASTM requirements at time of review.
