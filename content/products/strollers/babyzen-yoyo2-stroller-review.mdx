The **Babyzen YOYO2** is the answer to a specific question: is there a premium stroller that fits in an aircraft overhead bin? The answer is yes, and the YOYO2 is it. It folds to 52 × 44 × 18 cm — the same dimensions as a medium carry-on bag — and airlines including British Airways, Air France, and multiple US carriers allow it in the overhead bin. For families who travel by air with a young child, this is the central purchasing consideration.

## The Fold: How Small, How Fast, and What It Weighs

The YOYO2 fold is two-step: push a button on the handlebar, fold the frame. The process takes 3–4 seconds with one hand. At 13.6 lbs (6.2 kg), it's light enough to lift into an overhead bin single-handed. To put that weight in context — the Baby Jogger City Mini GT2 is 21.8 lbs and the UPPAbaby VISTA V2 is 26 lbs. When you're navigating an airport gate with a baby on one hip, 13.6 lbs versus 22 lbs is a meaningful difference every single time.

The folded stroller stands independently on the folded seat fabric — it won't tip over in an overhead bin or lean against a wall at the gate. The integrated carry strap lets you sling it over your shoulder or carry it like a handbag while pushing a second child, pulling luggage, or holding a carry-on. That carry strap sounds like a small detail until you're sprinting through an airport.

The unfolded dimensions are 40 × 17.5 × 42 inches — narrower than most umbrella strollers. Through restaurant aisles, museum hallways, and European shop doorways, this makes a practical difference. Most doorways in older European buildings are 27–29 inches wide; the YOYO2's 17.5-inch width clears them with margin.

## For Newborns: The 0+ Colour Pack

The YOYO2 frame alone cannot be used as a stroller — it requires a "colour pack" (seat fabric and attachment hardware) to become functional. For newborns, the 0+ colour pack positions the baby reclined in a nearly-flat, suitable position from birth. The recline angle is approximately 150 degrees — not fully flat, but appropriate for newborns per Babyzen's guidance. At approximately 6 months when the baby has head and trunk control, you swap to the 6+ colour pack for the upright forward-facing seat.

The cost reality is important to understand before you shop: the YOYO2 frame is ~$399 and each colour pack is ~$180–200. A full newborn-to-toddler setup costs $580–600 minimum — not $399 as the frame price implies. Some retailers bundle the frame and a 6+ pack at a modest discount. If you need the 0+ newborn pack separately, that's another $180 on top.

The colour pack attachment is tool-free but not intuitive the first time. Four clips, two per side, need to engage in sequence. After the third or fourth try it becomes second nature, but follow the video tutorial rather than trying to figure it out from the printed instructions.

## Handlebar Height: Who It Fits

The YOYO2's handlebar is fixed at 43.3 inches (110 cm) from the ground. For parents 5'5" to 5'10", this is comfortable. For parents over 6 feet, there can be slight stooping on long pushes. Babyzen does not offer a handlebar extension for the YOYO2 (unlike some competitors). If you're tall and plan extended daily pushing, try pushing the YOYO2 in a store before committing — for some taller parents it's fine; for others the fixed height is genuinely uncomfortable over distance.

## Ride Quality: Urban Performance on Real Streets

The YOYO2's suspension — four small foam-filled wheels with spring suspension — provides a smooth ride over pavement, tiled floors, and light urban surfaces. The front wheels are 6.5 inches in diameter; the rear wheels are 6.5 inches as well. These are smaller than all-terrain strollers (which run 8–12 inches) — that's the physical reason the YOYO2 folds as small as it does, and also the physical reason it handles rough terrain less well.

On smooth city sidewalks and mall floors, the ride is excellent — noticeably better than other ultra-compact strollers. Cobblestones and brick paths are manageable but you feel them. Grass paths, gravel trails, and anything unpaved aren't what this stroller is for.

Steering is genuinely light and responsive. One-handed pushing through crowds and tight shop spaces works well. The swivel front wheels can be locked in a forward position for higher-speed walking on open paths.

The seat when fully reclined allows sleeping toddlers to stay asleep through the transition from stroller to car — the smooth ride doesn't jolt them awake the way rougher compact strollers can.

## Boot Fit and Car Storage

In its folded state, the YOYO2 at 20 × 17.5 × 7 inches fits easily in the boot of virtually any car — including small city cars where full-size strollers are a squeeze. A Mini Cooper, Fiat 500, or Honda Jazz will hold the YOYO2 with room for bags. This makes it a practical everyday stroller even for parents who aren't flying — the compact fold is genuinely useful for car storage too, not just air travel.

The carry strap keeps the folded stroller under control when you're pulling it from the boot one-handed. Worth mentioning because with heavier strollers you're often using two hands just to lift and position, which requires setting the baby down first.

## YOYO2 vs. Doona Infant Car Seat Stroller

Both appeal to travel-focused parents. The core difference: the Doona is a car seat that rolls — it exists for car-to-destination transit and replaces the stroller need for the infant seat phase. The YOYO2 is a compact walking stroller for the 6-month-to-4-year window. They serve genuinely different use cases and most families who fly frequently want both: the Doona for the car-dependent newborn phase, the YOYO2 once the baby is past the infant seat stage.

## YOYO2 vs. Bugaboo Butterfly

The Bugaboo Butterfly ($599) is the closest direct competitor — also a compact fold, also overhead-bin compatible, also premium urban positioning. The Butterfly folds slightly smaller (17.5 × 23.5 × 10 inches) and has a better canopy with more coverage. The YOYO2 has a deeper accessory ecosystem and more extensive airline acceptance documentation.

For parents choosing between them: if canopy coverage is the priority, the Butterfly has a meaningful edge. If you want a broader accessories ecosystem (additional colour packs, YOYO+ newborn extension), the YOYO2 has more to offer. Both are excellent. At the same price point, the decision comes down to which limitations you'd rather live with.

## YOYO2 vs. Baby Jogger City Mini GT2

These don't really compete for the same parent — the GT2 is 21.8 lbs, doesn't fold for an overhead bin, and is built for all-terrain performance. The YOYO2 is 13.6 lbs, overhead-bin compatible, and built for city sidewalks and airports. If you're trying to choose between them, the question is: do you need all-terrain capability or travel compactness? If terrain, the GT2. If travel, the YOYO2. If both, buy the YOYO2 and accept the terrain limitation.

## Accessories and Ecosystem

The YOYO2 has the deepest accessory ecosystem in the compact stroller category:
- Rain cover (essential, and specifically fitted — third-party covers don't fit well)
- Stroller bag with shoulder strap (for checked luggage protection or carrying folded)
- YOYO+ bassinet for newborn airport pushchair use
- Car seat adapters for Cybex, Maxi-Cosi, Joie, Nuna, and others
- YOYO footmuff for cold weather
- Colour packs in 20+ options each season

The car seat adapter system is particularly useful for travel: snap a Maxi-Cosi or Nuna PIPA infant seat onto the YOYO2 frame and you have a functional travel system without a separate stroller. This setup carries the sleeping baby from the plane to the hotel without waking them.

## Canopy: Small But Functional

The YOYO2's canopy is smaller than full-size strollers. In direct afternoon sun, especially in summer or southern latitudes, a clip-on parasol (available from Babyzen and third parties) provides meaningful additional coverage. For typical European and North American city use, the standard canopy is adequate for most outings. The UPF 50+ rating is genuine — the fabric quality is good — but the coverage area is limited by the compact frame dimensions.

The canopy extends to a fixed second position but doesn't have the multi-panel telescoping extension that some full-size strollers offer. If you walk in direct sun for extended periods, budget for the parasol accessory.

## Long-Term Ownership: Colour Pack Wear and Frame Durability

The YOYO2's colour pack fabric shows wear faster than full-size stroller fabrics — the lighter woven construction catches abrasion from bags, car boots, and airport handling more visibly. After 18+ months of daily use, the colour pack typically shows fraying at the edges and fading in areas of contact. New colour packs cost $180–200, adding to the total cost of ownership over 3–4 years of use.

The frame itself is extremely durable. The aluminum construction and stainless hardware don't rust or degrade. Multiple families who travel frequently report YOYO2 frames surviving 100+ flights — gate checks, overhead bins, car boot use daily — without frame damage. The frame is the investment; the colour packs are consumables.

The most common mechanical issue: the front wheel bearings develop slight play after 18–24 months of heavy use. Babyzen sells replacement front wheel assemblies and they're straightforward to swap. This is a 10-minute fix that extends the frame's life significantly.

## Overhead Bin Reality Check

Most US domestic carriers permit the YOYO2 in overhead bins, but policy implementation varies by aircraft type and gate agent. The A320 family (United, American, Delta narrow-body) and Boeing 737 both have bins large enough for the folded YOYO2 in its standard orientation. The YOYO2 does not fit as a carry-on on some regional jets (CRJ-200, ERJ-145) where overhead bins are genuinely too small.

British Airways, Air France, KLM, Lufthansa, and most major European carriers explicitly list the YOYO2 as overhead-bin compatible in their policies. For US carriers, call ahead on the first flight — policies vary by route and aircraft assignment.

Carry the Babyzen travel bag on the first several flights. If a gate agent won't accept it overhead, the bag protects the frame from gate-check damage. After a few successful overhead flights, you'll know which routes and aircraft accept it without question.

## Getting the Best Price

The YOYO2 frame retails at $399. Colour packs are $180–200 each.

Some retailers bundle the frame with a 6+ colour pack at a ~$30 discount — check buy buy BABY and specialty baby stores for current bundles. The bundle is nearly always better value than buying separately.

Secondhand YOYO2 frames hold value well — expect $200–280 for a used frame in good condition. Buying a used frame and a new colour pack is a legitimate cost strategy that gets you to the full setup for $380–480 instead of $580–600.

The YOYO+ (previous generation) is still compatible with current colour packs and available at $50–80 discount at some retailers. The frame design is slightly older but functionally equivalent for most parents.

## Who Should NOT Buy the Babyzen YOYO2

**Parents who need all-terrain capability** — the 6.5-inch foam wheels handle city sidewalks but not gravel, grass, or rough trails. For terrain variety, the Baby Jogger City Mini GT2 with 12-inch rear wheels handles those surfaces properly.

**Suburban parents who primarily drive and don't fly** — the overhead-bin advantage is irrelevant if you're loading the stroller into a car boot. A full-size stroller delivers more storage, smoother rough-surface ride, and a bigger canopy at the same or lower price. The YOYO2's value proposition is specific to travel and urban compactness.

**Parents who need significant storage while walking** — the basket holds a small bag or a folded rain jacket, not a full diaper bag and groceries. Urban parents doing shop runs while pushing will need a bag on the handlebar or on their back.

**Very tall parents** — the fixed 43.3-inch handlebar is fine for average height but can cause back strain for parents over 6'1" over distance.

## What to Know Before You Buy

- **The YOYO2 frame alone is $399** — budget $580–600 for a usable birth-to-toddler setup including colour packs, not the frame price alone.
- **Verify your specific airline's policy before the first flight** — British Airways, Air France, KLM confirmed; US domestic varies by aircraft type.
- **The 0+ newborn pack uses different hardware than the 6+ pack** — all four attachment clips differ between packs. Follow the instruction video, not intuition.
- **Register for the Babyzen warranty within 30 days** of purchase — the 2-year frame warranty requires online registration.
- **The carry strap loop is rated for the full loaded stroller weight** — trust it in overhead bins.
