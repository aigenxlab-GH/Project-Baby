The **Babyzen YOYO2** answers a specific but increasingly common parent question: what do I do with a stroller when I'm on an airplane? The answer, until the YOYO2, was "gate-check it and hope it arrives undamaged." The YOYO2 changes that entirely.

## Carry-On Capable

Folded to 20 x 17.5 x 7 inches and weighing 13.6 lbs, the YOYO2 fits in the overhead bins of most commercial aircraft. You carry it on, stow it overhead, and retrieve it at your destination — no checking, no damage, no stroller waiting at the jet bridge. For parents who travel with children even twice a year, this alone justifies the price.

## City Stroller Performance

Beyond travel, the YOYO2 excels in dense cities. The compact fold means it fits in restaurant coat closets, elevator corners, and behind cafe tables. The light weight (13.6 lbs) makes one-handed carrying up subway stairs manageable. Despite its size, the four-wheel suspension absorbs urban terrain effectively.

## The Aluminum Frame

The YOYO2's frame is powder-coated aluminum — genuinely premium build quality that you feel immediately. No plastic creaks, no wobble. This stroller will outlast multiple children if cared for.

## Modular Accessories

Babyzen sells a newborn pack (extends to birth), color packs (swappable fabrics in 20+ colors), and a rain cover. The modular system lets you customize the stroller for different seasons and uses.

## What You Give Up

Storage is minimal — a small basket under the seat that holds a wallet and snacks but not a diaper bag. This is the trade-off for the compact size. Plan to use a backpack or attach a bag to the handlebar.
