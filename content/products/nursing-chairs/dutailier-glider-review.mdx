## Buying Guide  
When selecting a nursing chair, prioritize **durability** and **ergonomics** to ensure comfort during long feeding sessions. The chair should support your back and encourage a natural posture to avoid strain. A **weight capacity of at least 250 lbs** is essential for most parents, and **adjustable height** can make it easier to access a changing table or crib.  

Look for a **locking mechanism**—whether a multi-position lock or a fixed position—to stabilize the chair when transferring a sleeping baby. The **glide or swivel motion** should be smooth and silent, avoiding jerky movements that might disturb infants. **Cushioning quality** is another key factor: high-density foam or memory foam retains shape longer than cheaper, lower-density alternatives.  

For safety, ensure the chair is **sturdy and stable**, with a wide base to prevent tipping. Avoid models with sharp edges or loose parts that could pose hazards to curious toddlers. **Portability** is often overlooked but important for parents who need to adjust their workspace or share the chair with other caregivers.  

Common mistakes include underestimating the chair’s weight or assuming a single-use chair will suffice. Families with multiple children may regret buying a budget model that degrades quickly. Conversely, those with limited storage space might not account for the chair’s bulkiness. Always test the chair in person if possible, or read detailed reviews that align with your needs.
