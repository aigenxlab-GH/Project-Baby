**The Summer Infant Pop 'n Sit Portable Booster Seat** is a must-have for parents seeking a budget-friendly, portable solution to keep babies safely seated during meals. Designed for infants aged 4 months to 3 years, this lightweight booster seat folds completely flat, fits in a diaper bag, and securely attaches to most standard chairs. With a 4.3-star rating and a score of 8.3/10, it balances affordability with functionality, making it ideal for families who prioritize convenience without sacrificing safety.

## Design & Build  
The Pop ‘n Sit’s design prioritizes portability and practicality. Weighing just 1.5 pounds, it’s made from durable, lightweight plastic and padded fabric that feels sturdy yet comfortable. The seat’s compact folded size (12x12x3 inches) ensures it fits easily into a tote bag or carry-on, while the adjustable strap system accommodates chairs with seat thicknesses up to 3 inches. A three-point harness secures the child, and the removable, dishwasher-safe tray is a thoughtful addition for messy meals. While aesthetics are minimal—offering neutral colors and a streamlined look—the materials hold up well over time, resisting warping or cracks. The seat’s open sides allow easy access for caregivers but may feel less enclosed for fussy babies. Overall, it’s a no-frills design that delivers on its core promise: portability and safety.

## Performance  
The Pop ‘n Sit excels at its primary function: keeping babies upright and secure during meals. The three-point harness prevents slouching or slipping, while the strap system—featuring a wide adjustable band and a secondary buckle—creates a stable connection to chairs with standard seat heights. Parents report it works reliably at home and in restaurants, though thicker or curved seats (like those in some cafes) may pose challenges. The tray’s 8.5x9 inch surface area is ample for small plates or utensils, but its low profile means taller cups or large bowls may tip. For infants under 5 months, the lack of head support can cause younger babies to slump; however, its weight limit of 37 lbs supports toddlers through their busy eating phase. Cleanup is a breeze, with the tray and seat surface swiping clean in seconds. While not as stable as a dedicated high chair, its compact size and secure attachment make it a practical alternative in tight spaces or on-the-go.

## What We Like  
1. **Flat-Fold Portability**: Parents traveling frequently praise the Pop ‘n Sit’s ability to fold flat in seconds. One user shared how it fits neatly into their carry-on for airport meals, avoiding the hassle of restaurant high chairs.  
2. **Secure Attachment**: The adjustable strap system works wonders in most home dining chairs and café seats. A parent with a large family noted how it eliminates the need for bulky portable high chairs during weekend trips.  
3. **Easy Cleanup**: The dishwasher-safe tray is a lifesaver after pureed food or finger-feeding messes. A first-time mom raved about how it simplified midday meals at park picnics.  

## Drawbacks  
1. **Tray Size Limitations**: The tray’s modest dimensions can’t accommodate larger items like sandwich halves or bulky utensils, requiring frequent repositioning.  
2. **Chair Compatibility Issues**: While most chairs work well, parents with home recliners or restaurant chairs with thick padding report struggles with the straps slipping. A quick check of chair thickness before travel is essential.  
3. **Limited Head Support for Younger Babies**: Infants under 5 months may need additional head propping, which the Pop ‘n Sit lacks. Some parents use rolled towels or baby carriers for extra support during early meals.  

## Buying Guide  
When choosing a booster seat like the Pop ‘n Sit, consider:  
- **Age/Weight Suitability**: Ensure your child falls within the 4-month to 3-year range or 37 lbs weight limit.  
- **Chair Compatibility**: Measure your home dining or restaurant chairs to confirm the seat thickness fits the 3-inch maximum.  
- **Safety Priorities**: Opt for models with a three-point harness (vs. a single strap) for secure seating. Always test the attachment system before use.  
- **Portability Needs**: If you travel often, prioritize seats that fold flat and weigh under 2 pounds. Avoid models with bulky bases.  
- **Common Mistakes**: Don’t use the Pop ‘n Sit on wobbly chairs or in standing positions (it’s designed for seated use only). Also, avoid overloading the tray to prevent balance issues.  

## Verdict  
**Score: 8.3/10**  
The Summer Infant Pop ‘n Sit is a top pick for families who value portability and convenience. Ideal for frequent travelers, small households, or parents who meal-time flexibility, it delivers on its promise of a secure, foldable feeding solution. However, those needing a larger tray, sturdier home-use high chair, or head support for younger infants may find its limitations frustrating. For its $35 price point, it’s an excellent value—just pair it with a headrest for babies under 5 months and be mindful of chair thickness for optimal use.
