The **Stokke Tripp Trapp** has been continuously manufactured since 1972 — over 50 years — with the same fundamental design. In a market where most baby products are engineered for obsolescence, the Tripp Trapp is an anomaly: it genuinely improves with each iteration while remaining backward compatible.

## The Design Innovation That Changed High Chairs

In 1972, designer Peter Opsvik recognized that conventional high chairs positioned children beside the family table rather than at it — creating a separateness at mealtimes. The Tripp Trapp was designed so the child sits at table level, part of the family meal, with proper foot support and back support at their actual scale.

This sounds simple. It was revolutionary. The principle remains the basis of pediatric occupational therapy recommendations for mealtime positioning today.

## From 6 Months to 330 Pounds

With the Baby Set attached, the Tripp Trapp accommodates infants from 6 months with a 5-point harness. Remove the Baby Set and the chair becomes a normal-height child's seat. Lower the seat and footboard rails to adult proportions and it's a fully functional adult dining chair rated to 330 lbs.

Many Stokke owners use the same chair for multiple children, and some use them for decades as adult desk or dining chairs.

## The Economics of Premium

The Tripp Trapp retails for ~$330. Add the Baby Set (~$75) and a cushion (~$50) and you're at ~$455. That's significant. But Tripp Trapp chairs in good condition routinely sell secondhand for $150–250 — meaning resale recovers much of the cost. And as a chair used daily for 10–15 years, the per-use cost is fractions of a cent.
