**The **Evenflo Position and Lock Tall Gate** is a mid-range pressure-mount solution designed for parents navigating non-parallel or angled walls. Its standout feature is the “position and lock” mechanism, which adjusts to irregular wall angles, making it ideal for older homes or tricky installations. At $45, it balances affordability and functionality, though it’s not suited for stairways.**

## Design & Build  
Crafted from durable steel, the Evenflo Position and Lock Tall Gate is built to withstand daily wear from curious toddlers. The matte black finish resists fingerprints and scuffing, though it may show dust over time. The gate’s dimensions—30 inches tall and adjustable from 29 to 42 inches wide—make it suitable for most standard doorways and hallways. What sets it apart is the “position and lock” system: two pressure-mounted spindles that can be independently adjusted to fit walls that are not perfectly straight. This feature is a lifesaver for homes with older, crooked doorways or uneven walls, which are common in pre-1950s construction.  

The gate’s design prioritizes function over aesthetics, with a streamlined, no-frills look that blends into most home decors. Unlike premium models with ergonomic handles or soft-touch latches, the Evenflo relies on a simple, mechanical lock system. While this keeps costs down, it also means the gate feels utilitarian rather than premium. The steel construction adds weight and stability, reducing the risk of tipping when a toddler leans or pushes against it. However, heavier models like the Summer Infant 1261 may offer a more polished appearance.  

## Performance  
The Evenflo gate shines in its primary role: containing toddlers in doorways with irregular angles. Parents with older homes or non-standard layouts often struggle to install gates that don’t gap or wobble, but the positioning system ensures a secure fit. For example, one reviewer with a 1930s house successfully installed it in a doorway that sloped outward, a challenge for most competitors. The 30-inch height is effective for toddlers up to 18 months, though athletic 18–24-month-olds might attempt to climb over.  

The pressure mount is robust, gripping door frames firmly without damaging walls—a must for renters or those with delicate surfaces. For wider openings, the 29–42-inch base can be extended with 12-inch add-ons (sold separately), making it adaptable for hallways or large rooms. However, the gate’s rigidity means it lacks the one-handed operation of some hardware-mounted models, requiring both hands to adjust the positioning spindles. A common user tip is to test the gate’s stability after installation by pushing it sideways to ensure it remains secure.  

## What We Like  
1. **Angle-Adapting Positioning**: A parent with a 1920s home used the Evenflo gate to block a doorway with a 15-degree slant. After adjusting the spindles, the gate fit snugly without gaps, a feat impossible with standard models.  
2. **Wide Span Flexibility**: Families with multiple doorways can use a single gate across the 29–42-inch range. One user installed it in a hallway and later moved it to a laundry room, saving $30 on a second purchase.  
3. **Wall-Friendly Design**: A renter installed the gate without damage, later removing it to leave the walls pristine. The pressure mount’s rubber pads prevented scuffing on painted concrete.  

## Drawbacks  
1. **No Stair Use**: Unlike the Regalo Pressure Mount Gate (which has a stair-safe hardware option), the Evenflo is strictly for wall-mounted doorways. A parent with a two-story home needed a separate gate for stairs, doubling their cost.  
2. **Adjustment Learning Curve**: New users often spend 10–15 minutes fine-tuning the spindles for angled walls. One reviewer noted that the instructions lack visual aids, leading to initial confusion.  
3. **Less One-Handed Operation**: Parents with small children in one arm may find the dual-spindle adjustment cumbersome. The Summer Infant 1261, for instance, features a one-handed lever for easier access.  

## Safe Use & Setup  
Proper installation is critical for safety. Start by measuring your doorway precisely; the Evenflo gate spans 29–42 inches. For angled walls, use a level to identify the slope and adjust the spindles accordingly. Push the gate into the doorway, then twist the spindles until resistance is firm but not excessive. Test the gate by applying lateral pressure—any movement indicates a poor fit.  

Always install the gate **at least 6 inches below the wall** to prevent toddlers from climbing over the top. Avoid using it on stairs or uneven surfaces. For added security, place a rubber stopper under the gate’s base to prevent slipping. Recheck the fit weekly, as pressure mounts can shift over time. Never use the gate if the locking mechanism feels loose or the hardware is damaged.  

## How It Compares  
The Evenflo competes with models like the **Summer Infant 1261** and **Regalo Pressure Mount Gate**. The Summer Infant offers a 36-inch height and one-handed operation, making it better for older toddlers but pricier at $60. The Regalo gate includes a stair-safe hardware kit for $55, ideal for multi-level homes.  

Where the Evenflo excels is in its angle-adapting design—a feature absent in most competitors. The Regalo’s hardware kit is rigid and less flexible for angled walls, while the Summer Infant lacks the 29–42-inch adjustability. For budget-conscious parents with non-standard doorways, the Evenflo is a cost-effective solution. However, those prioritizing stair safety or one-handed use should consider alternatives.  

## Verdict  
The Evenflo Position and Lock Tall Gate scores **7.8/10**, earning 4 stars for its innovative angle-adapting design. It’s a **top choice for parents with non-parallel walls or irregular doorways**, offering reliable performance at a reasonable price. However, it falls short for stairways and requires more setup effort than competitors. Families prioritizing **ease of installation** or **one-handed operation** might prefer the Summer Infant or Regalo models. If your home has standard, straight walls, consider a narrower or taller gate for better value.
