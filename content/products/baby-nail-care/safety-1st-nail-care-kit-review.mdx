## Why Rounded-Tip Scissors Are the First Choice

Pediatricians who are consulted about newborn nail care frequently recommend scissors over clippers for the first 3 months. The reason is control. Clippers have a spring mechanism that executes the cut once you exceed a pressure threshold. You don't control the exact moment of cutting — the mechanism does.

Scissors require deliberate positioning and a deliberate cutting motion. You see both blade jaws. You have complete control over the cut timing and depth. For tiny newborn nails, this control matters.

The rounded tips on the Safety 1st scissors eliminate the theoretical risk of a sharp point accidentally poking baby. Between the rounded design and the full control of scissors, this is the mechanism that pediatricians most frequently recommend for newborns specifically.

## The Brush Function Explained

Baby nail beds accumulate lint and debris in the slight curl that newborn nails naturally have. This is not unusual or unsafe — it's just what happens when there's a tiny space under a curved nail. The brush gently cleans this space, reducing the buildup and improving nail health.
