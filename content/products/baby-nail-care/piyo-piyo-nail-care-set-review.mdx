## The Sharpness Standard

Why does blade sharpness matter so much for baby nails? Because the cutting mechanism is fundamentally different between sharp and dull blades.

A sharp blade cuts cleanly. It severs the nail with a clean edge. The nail edge is smooth and intact.

A dull blade doesn't cut — it crushes and tears. The compression force of the blade squeezes the nail, forcing it apart rather than cleanly cutting it. The result is a crushed, torn edge.

These torn edges are the problem. Frayed edges catch on fabric. They peel as the nail grows out. They're uncomfortable. They require more frequent filing to smooth. They sometimes cause small scratches as the jagged edge catches baby skin.

The Piyo Piyo's sharper stainless blades produce the clean-cut outcome. You trim once, the edge is smooth, you're done. Less follow-up filing needed. Better outcome for baby.

## The Grip Ergonomics

Soft-grip handles accomplish two things: better control and better feedback. When you hold a tool with a hard plastic handle, there's a disconnect between your hand and the tool. Your grip has to be tight to maintain control. You can't feel subtle feedback about what the tool is doing.

Soft-grip handles create contact. Your fingers can sense the tool's behavior more directly. You have finer control over the cutting action. For a task that requires precision (baby nail trimming), that finer control matters.

## Soft-Grip Practical Impact

In practical terms, with soft-grip handles you can execute precise cuts more easily. The tool responds to your intentions more directly. You can stop the cutting action more immediately if needed.
