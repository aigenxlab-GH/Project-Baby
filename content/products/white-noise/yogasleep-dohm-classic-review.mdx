The **Yogasleep Dohm Classic** has been soothing babies — and their exhausted parents — since 1962. That's not a marketing claim: this exact product, with a real spinning fan inside, has been sold virtually unchanged for over 60 years.

## What Makes It Different

Every other white noise machine (including the excellent LectroFan) generates sound digitally through a speaker. The Dohm is different: a real fan spins inside a two-speed motor housing, and the sound of that spinning fan is what comes out. No speakers, no audio files, no digital processing.

For many people, this produces a more natural, room-filling sound that feels less "electronic" than speaker-generated noise.

## Adjustment Is Physical

To change volume, you twist the outer housing away from you (louder) or toward you (quieter). To change tone, you rotate the cap to expose more or fewer vent holes — open vents create brighter sound; closed vents create fuller, deeper sound. There's no app, no buttons, no remote. Some parents love this simplicity; others find it limiting.

## The Limitations

The Dohm only makes one basic type of sound: fan noise. It doesn't do ocean waves, rainfall, or lullabies. If you want variety, look at the LectroFan or Hatch Rest. It also runs AC-only — no USB or battery option, so travel requires a power adapter.

## Who It's For

Parents who want the most natural-sounding white noise possible, who prefer physical controls over apps, and who value time-tested simplicity. The Dohm is also a favorite with adults who want to use it as a sleep aid long after baby grows up.
