The BabyBjörn Potty Chair excels in its primary function: making potty training as efficient and stress-free as possible. The standout feature is the removable inner bucket. Safety and durability are also strong suits. The potty’s rounded edges and stable base prevent tipping, even when a toddler rocks or shifts position. The high splash guard directs liquid toward the center, reducing spillage—a boon for parents dealing with squirting streams during cleanup. Despite regular use over months, the materials show no signs of warping or cracking, ensuring longevity.

## Safe Use & Setup  
Setting up the BabyBjörn Potty Chair requires attention to stability and safety. Begin by placing the potty on a flat, non-slip surface—carpet or a rubber mat helps prevent sliding. Ensure the inner bucket is securely seated in the base to avoid wobbling. For younger or less steady toddlers, consider placing the potty near a wall or furniture for added support during use.  

Safety is paramount: always supervise your child during potty use, especially in the early stages of training. The potty’s rounded edges reduce the risk of injury if a child falls, but avoid leaving young users unattended. For cleaning, use mild soap and water to rinse the inner bucket immediately after each use. Disinfect weekly with a diluted bleach solution (1:16 ratio) to maintain hygiene, but avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the plastic.  

When transitioning to the toilet, teach your child to hold the potty firmly with both hands for balance. Never let them climb on the potty, and keep it out of reach during non-use to prevent accidental tipping. By following these guidelines, you’ll maximize the potty’s safety and effectiveness throughout your child’s training journey.

## Buying Guide  
When selecting a potty chair, consider your child’s age, physical development, and your own cleaning preferences. Most children start potty training between 18–24 months, but some may need a potty earlier if they show interest. Look for a potty with a seat height that allows feet to rest flat on the floor—this prevents straining and ensures proper elimination.  

Safety is paramount. Avoid potties with plastic that smells chemical or has a soft, spongy texture, as these may contain harmful additives. BPA-free and phthalate-free materials are non-negotiable. For hygiene, prioritize potties with smooth, crevice-free surfaces that resist bacterial growth.  

A common mistake is choosing a potty that’s too small or too large for your child. The BabyBjörn’s size suits most toddlers, but very small children may need a compact model. Another oversight is ignoring the cleaning process—opt for removable parts or easy-empty systems to reduce daily hassle. Lastly, consider future transitions: some potties double as a step stool or include a reducer insert, which can save money long-term.
