The **Boppy Original Nursing Pillow** has sold over 10 million units and has been a staple of maternity wards and lactation consultants' offices for decades. We've tested it across multiple feeding stages, and here's the honest assessment.

## How the Boppy Supports Nursing

The C-shaped design wraps around your waist, providing a stable platform at the right height to bring baby to breast without hunching forward. This takes enormous strain off your neck, back, and arms during what can be 8–12 feeding sessions per day in the newborn weeks.

## Beyond Breastfeeding

What makes the Boppy outlast the nursing stage is its versatility:

- **Tummy time**: Prop baby over the pillow for supported tummy time — they can look around while building neck strength
- **Supported sitting**: As baby approaches 4–6 months, use the Boppy as a prop ring to help them practice sitting upright
- **Positioning**: Useful for keeping baby propped at a safe angle after feeding to reduce spit-up

## Washing and Maintenance

The slipcover removes easily and is machine washable — critical since newborns produce truly impressive amounts of spit-up and leaking. Buy a second cover so you always have a clean one ready. The inner pillow can also be machine washed on gentle cycle.

## Who It's Best For

The Boppy fits most body types but works best for parents who are average or slightly above average height. Petite parents may find it too wide; larger parents may find it doesn't quite reach. In those cases, consider the Boppy Custom Fit or the My Brest Friend pillow as alternatives.

## Safety Standards & Certifications

Breast pump accessories and nursing products that contact milk must be **BPA-free, phthalate-free**, and food-contact safe per **FDA 21 CFR**. Silicone manual pumps carry no formal safety certification requirements, but reputable brands use food-grade silicone compliant with FDA food contact standards. Replace silicone membranes and valves every 2–3 months as microscopic tears reduce suction efficiency.
