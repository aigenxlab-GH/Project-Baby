**## Safe Use & Setup**  
Proper setup is critical for maximizing the Philips Avent Anti-Colic Bottle’s effectiveness. Begin by **assembling the bottle correctly**: insert the AirFree valve into the bottle body first, then secure the cap. The valve must sit flush against the bottle wall to function—misalignment can allow air to enter the nipple. For newborns, test the flow by tilting the bottle at a 45-degree angle during feeding to ensure the valve remains submerged in liquid.  

**Sterilization is non-negotiable** for infants under six months. Use a steam sterilizer or a dedicated bottle sterilizing tablet, as the AirFree valve’s silicone parts can trap bacteria. Dishwasher-safe components simplify cleaning, but avoid hand-washing with harsh scrubbing, which can wear down the valve’s silicone over time. Always disassemble the bottle after each use to prevent milk residue buildup in the valve’s crevices.  

For safety, **never use the bottle with hot liquids**. The AirFree valve isn’t heatproof and could melt at temperatures above 160°F. When feeding, hold the bottle upright to maintain valve alignment and reduce the risk of air bubbles. Parents of fast-paced feeders should consider using a **slow-flow nipple** to prevent choking and ensure the valve has time to channel air properly.  

**Storage tips**: While the wide neck makes cleaning easier, it also takes up more space in diaper bags. Opt for a bottle bag with a dedicated compartment to prevent the bottle from knocking against other items, which can dislodge the valve. Replace the AirFree valve every 3–6 months, as silicone parts degrade with use.
