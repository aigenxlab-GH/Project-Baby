The **Haakaa Silicone Breast Pump Gen 2** is one of those rare products that does exactly one thing and does it brilliantly. It has become a staple recommendation from lactation consultants worldwide — and at $30, it's genuinely transformative for breastfeeding mothers.

## The Letdown Problem It Solves

When your baby latches and begins nursing, your body releases milk (letdown) from both breasts simultaneously — not just the one being nursed. Most of that letdown from the other breast soaks into a breast pad and is wasted. The Haakaa attaches to the unoccupied breast and catches that milk passively.

## How to Use It

1. Squeeze the pump body gently
2. Attach to your breast to create suction
3. Continue nursing your baby normally on the other side
4. Collect, store, or discard the milk after feeding

The Gen 2 adds a suction base so it doesn't tip over if you set it down mid-session. This sounds minor until you've spilled an ounce of collected breast milk on the sofa.

## Building a Freezer Stash

The true value of the Haakaa is cumulative. If you collect 1 oz per nursing session, and nurse 8 times a day, that's 8 oz of stored milk per day with zero extra pumping time. Most breastfeeding parents use the Haakaa to build a freezer stash in the first weeks without any additional effort.

## What It Cannot Do

The Haakaa is not an electric pump. It cannot empty your breast, which is necessary to maintain supply when separated from baby. It doesn't work while you're not actively nursing. For going back to work or pumping independently, you still need an electric pump.
