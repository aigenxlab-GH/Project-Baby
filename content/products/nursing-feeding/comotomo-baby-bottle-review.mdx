The **Comotomo Baby Bottle** has achieved near-legendary status among breastfeeding parents — particularly those whose babies have refused every other bottle. After testing it alongside 7 other bottles, we understand why lactation consultants have made it their top recommendation.

## The Design Philosophy

Where most baby bottles are rigid plastic cylinders, Comotomo is soft, squeezable silicone that feels remarkably similar to breast tissue. The nipple is wide and dome-shaped, requiring the same wide latch as breastfeeding. The soft body allows baby to grip it and even squeeze it slightly — another breast-like behavior.

This isn't just marketing. The physical similarity to a breast is measurably different from other bottles, and it shows in how breastfed babies respond to it.

## The Anti-Colic Venting

Dual anti-colic vents on either side of the nipple allow air to enter the bottle to replace milk as baby feeds. This keeps the nipple from collapsing (which causes babies to suck harder and ingest more air) and reduces gas.

## Easy Cleaning

The wide neck is large enough to fit an adult hand inside for thorough washing — no specialized bottle brushes needed. Dishwasher and microwave safe. The silicone doesn't harbor bacteria the way soft plastic can over time.

## When to Use It

Introduce the Comotomo between 3–6 weeks of age when breastfeeding is established. Any earlier, and bottle preference may interfere with learning to nurse. Any later, and many babies become resistant to bottles entirely.
