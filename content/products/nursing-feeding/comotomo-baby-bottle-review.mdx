## Nipple Flow Rates: Start Here

Before getting into any specific bottle, understand nipple flow rates — because getting this wrong causes as many feeding problems as bottle choice does.

**Slow flow (Level 1):** Designed for newborns from 0–3 months. Requires active sucking effort. Mirrors the work of breastfeeding. Starting a newborn on medium or fast flow means milk arrives faster than they can swallow, leading to gulping, air ingestion, and colic symptoms.

**Medium flow (Level 2):** Typically for 3–6 months as the baby's sucking strength increases and they can manage faster flow without gulping.

**Fast flow (Level 3+):** For 6+ months or formula-fed babies who need faster flow. Y-cut nipples handle thicker liquids.

The rule: start slow, move up only when the baby is working noticeably hard to get milk and taking much longer than usual for their feeds. An underchallenged baby gulps and ingests air. An overchallenged baby gets frustrated and refuses the bottle. Match the flow to their developmental stage, not to their impatience.

Comotomo offers slow, medium, and fast flow nipples. Start with slow for any baby under 3 months.

## When to Introduce

Introduce the Comotomo between 3–6 weeks of age when breastfeeding is established. Any earlier, and bottle preference may interfere with learning to nurse. Any later, and many babies become resistant to bottles entirely.

The timing matters. Breastfeeding parents who plan to eventually use bottles — for returning to work, for partner feeds, for building a freezer stash — need to introduce a bottle in this narrow window. Missing it creates a much harder problem to solve at 3 months.

## Sterilisation and BPA-Free Materials

BPA (bisphenol A) is a plasticizer that has been shown to disrupt hormones. The FDA banned it from baby bottles and sippy cups in 2012. All reputable bottle brands — including Comotomo — are BPA-free by default. Phthalate-free is the next standard to look for; Comotomo's food-grade silicone contains no phthalates.

Silicone sterilises more easily than plastic. Microwave steam steriliser, electric steam steriliser, boiling water — all work with Comotomo. Dishwasher safe on the top rack. The wide neck means no specialized bottle brush is needed; an adult hand fits inside for manual washing.

Replace the nipple when it shows any signs of thinning, stickiness, or discolouration — approximately every 2–3 months with daily use.

## Anti-Colic Venting: How the Dual Vents Work

Comotomo's dual anti-colic vents are positioned on either side of the nipple. As baby feeds, air enters through the vents to replace the milk, preventing negative pressure (vacuum) from building. Without venting, the nipple collapses, baby sucks harder, ingests more air, and becomes gassy and uncomfortable.

The Comotomo system is less complex than Dr. Brown's internal vent tube — fewer parts, simpler cleaning. It is effective for most babies. For babies with significant colic or reflux where maximum air reduction is needed, Dr. Brown's vent system tends to perform better due to its more complete air bypass.

The two most commonly recommended bottles for breastfed babies or colic-prone babies are Comotomo and Dr. Brown's Natural Flow.

## The Opaque Body: Is It a Real Problem?

The colored silicone (green or pink) body is semi-opaque, making fill levels harder to read than clear plastic bottles. Measurement markings are embossed but not as crisp as printed markings on clear plastic.

In practice: most parents who use Comotomo regularly adapt to estimating by feel and weight within a week. For parents who need precise measurements for medical or supply-tracking reasons, using a kitchen scale to weigh milk by volume (1ml breast milk ≈ 1g) is more accurate than any bottle marking anyway.

## BPA-Free and Beyond: What to Check

All major bottle brands are BPA-free. When comparing bottles for safety:

- **Silicone** (Comotomo): BPA-free, phthalate-free, no leaching under heat. Preferred for parents minimizing plastic contact.
- **Polypropylene** (Dr. Brown's, Avent): BPA-free, FDA-approved food contact material. Standard for plastic bottles.
- **Glass** (Philips Avent glass, Lifefactory): Zero plastic contact concerns, heavier and breakable.

Comotomo's 100% silicone body and nipple provides the cleanest materials profile of any major bottle brand, which is why it consistently appears on lists for chemically cautious parents.
