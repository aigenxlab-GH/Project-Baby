**Green Toys Tug Boat Review: A Durable, Eco-Conscious Bath Toy for Discerning Parents**  
The **Green Toys Tug Boat** is ideal for parents prioritizing safety, sustainability, and longevity in bath-time essentials. Made from 100% recycled milk jugs, it’s free from BPA, phthalates, and PVC, offering a non-toxic, mold-resistant toy that withstands years of daily use. While its minimalist design may not appeal to all, this eco-friendly bath toy earns high marks for durability and ethical manufacturing.

## Design & Build: Clean Simplicity Meets Practicality  
The Tug Boat’s design is straightforward yet functional. Measuring approximately 10 inches long, its streamlined shape fits comfortably in a child’s hand, while the hollow hull allows it to float effortlessly. The single green color (in standard versions) is integrated into the plastic itself, avoiding surface coatings that can chip or fade. The boat’s smooth, rounded edges eliminate sharp corners, reducing the risk of nicks during bath time.  

Constructed from 100% recycled HDPE (high-density polyethylene), the material feels sturdy and slightly textured, resisting slipping even when wet. Unlike many bath toys, the Tug Boat’s hollow design includes a small drain hole, ensuring complete water drainage and preventing mold. This attention to detail reflects Green Toys’ focus on practicality—parents can trust this toy to dry quickly and maintain hygiene. The lack of moving parts or intricate features adds to its durability, making it ideal for toddlers who may toss or dunk it repeatedly.

## Performance: Built to Last, Designed to Stay Safe  
The Green Toys Tug Boat excels as a no-frills bath companion. Its primary function—floating reliably in water—is supported by the lightweight yet robust HDPE material. Parents report using it for years without cracks or warping, even after daily exposure to chlorinated or saltwater pools. The drain hole and hollow construction eliminate the mold and mildew risks common in sealed plastic toys, a major plus for humid bathrooms.

The boat’s durability also reduces the need for frequent replacements, cutting down on waste. Dishwasher-safe cleaning is another convenience, though it’s not necessary thanks to the self-draining design.

## Safe Use & Setup: Practical Tips for Parents  
To maximize the Tug Boat’s safety and longevity, proper setup and care are essential. Begin by supervising your child during bath time, as even non-toxic toys can pose risks if not used appropriately. For infants (6–12 months), ensure they don’t mouth the toy excessively; while the HDPE is safe, continuous chewing can wear down edges over time.  

Clean the boat after each use by rinsing it with warm water and a mild detergent. The hollow design allows for thorough drying—place it upright in a ventilated area to air dry completely, preventing moisture buildup. Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive scrubbers, which can degrade the plastic.  

Storage is equally important. After drying, keep the toy in a dry, cool location. Avoid storing it in sealed containers, as trapped humidity can lead to mold. The built-in drain hole helps, but occasional checks for residual water are recommended. For toddlers, consider pairing the boat with bath-safe activities like filling and emptying it with water to encourage motor skills.
