The **Nanit Pro** changed what parents expect from a baby monitor. It's not just a camera —  it's a sleep coach. Here's whether the premium price is justified.

## Video Quality

The 1080p HD video with automatic night vision is the best we've seen in a baby monitor. The image is sharp enough to clearly see your baby's face and breathing in complete darkness.

## Sleep Tracking

This is Nanit's killer feature. The overhead camera tracks when your baby falls asleep, how many times they wake, and how long each sleep session lasts. Over time, it builds a sleep history you can share with your pediatrician.

## Breathing Motion Monitoring

With the Nanit Breathing Wear (a swaddle or sleep sack with a subtle pattern), the camera tracks breathing motion and alerts you if it stops detecting movement. This gives many parents significant peace of mind.

## The Subscription Question

The $99/year subscription unlocks sleep insights, expert sleep tips, and milestone photos. Without it, you still get live HD video. For most parents, the subscription pays for itself in reduced pediatrician calls about sleep concerns.

## Safety Standards & Certifications

Baby monitors are regulated by the **FCC** for radio frequency emissions. Video monitors sold in the US must meet FCC Part 15 requirements. For WiFi monitors, look for **WPA2/WPA3 encryption** and two-factor authentication to prevent unauthorized access. Audio monitors carry no child-specific safety standards — the safety guarantee is in the connection reliability, not the device itself.
