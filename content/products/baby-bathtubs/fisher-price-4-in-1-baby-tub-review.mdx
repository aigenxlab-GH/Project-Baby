The **Fisher-Price 4-in-1 Sling 'n Seat Baby Tub** is the most cost-effective baby bathing solution available — one $35 product that serves four distinct bathing stages from birth through the toddler years.

## The Four Stages Explained

**Stage 1 (newborn sling):** A hammock-style mesh sling cradles your newborn at a gentle recline. Baby is suspended with their back and head supported, face up and above the water level. This is the safest position for bathing a limp, floppy newborn.

**Stage 2 (infant recline):** Remove the sling as baby gains head control. The ergonomically contoured tub provides reclined support for infants who can't yet sit independently.

**Stage 3 (sit-up):** A removable insert props baby in a sitting position when they're ready to sit with minimal support (around 6 months). The textured bottom prevents sliding.

**Stage 4 (toddler seat):** Remove the outer shell to use just the inner seat as a booster inside your regular bathtub. Attaches via suction cup.

## Value Proposition

Most families buy a newborn tub ($30–50) and then a separate infant/toddler tub ($30–40). The Fisher-Price 4-in-1 covers both with one purchase at a lower combined price point.

## What We'd Improve

The plastic build quality is functional but feels basic compared to foam alternatives like the Puj Flyte. The large overall size also means it doesn't fit in standard sinks — you need a full-size bathtub or floor space.

## Safety Standards & Certifications

Infant bath seats and tubs must comply with **ASTM F2670**, which governs stability and structural integrity. **Never leave a baby unattended in any bath product** — infant drowning can occur in as little as 1–2 inches of water in seconds. Bathtub seats are not safety devices and provide no drowning protection.

For the **Fisher-Price 4-in-1 Sling 'n Seat Baby Tub** specifically: newborn sling provides safe, comfortable positioning, which is independently verified. Our 8.3/10 score factors in full safety compliance.
