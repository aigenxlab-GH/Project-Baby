**The **Primo EuroBath Plus Baby Bathtub** is a budget-friendly, ergonomic solution for parents seeking a safe, anatomically correct bathing experience for newborns to toddlers. Designed for easy one-handed use, it supports babies in a natural reclined position that reduces neck strain and adapts to growing needs with two distinct ends. Ideal for families prioritizing comfort, longevity, and a no-frills approach to infant care.**

## Design & Build  
The EuroBath Plus is constructed from durable, BPA-free plastic, ensuring safety and resilience for daily use. Its 4.5-inch depth retains water temperature better than flatter alternatives, while the non-tip base—an essential safety feature—prevents accidental tipping during use. The tub’s dual ends are its standout design element: the newborn end (for 0–6 months) features a fully reclined contour with elevated head support, while the toddler end (for 6–24 months) offers a semi-upright seat with higher backrests. Aesthetically, the tub has a minimalist, European-inspired look with smooth edges and a matte finish that resists slipping. At 25.6 inches long and 17.7 inches wide, it’s compact enough for most bathtubs but too large for standard single-bowl sinks—a limitation to note. The non-removable insert, while simplifying assembly, requires thorough cleaning of the entire unit. Weighing just under 2 pounds, it’s lightweight for handling but sturdy enough to support a 25-pound toddler. The design prioritizes function over flashy extras, making it a practical choice for parents seeking a no-hassle, long-lasting tub.  

## Performance  
The EuroBath Plus excels in its core purpose: providing a secure, ergonomic bathing experience. The reclined newborn position mimics a baby’s natural spinal curve, reducing strain on their developing neck muscles. This is a critical advantage over flat-tub designs, which can cause discomfort or instability. The dual-end system extends usability beyond 24 months, doubling its value compared to single-configuration tubs. The 4.5-inch depth proved ideal in testing: warm water stays hot longer than in shallow tubs, reducing the need for constant reheating. The non-tip base, with its wide, textured feet, kept the tub stable even when a toddler shifted positions—a common issue with cheaper models. However, the lack of a built-in rinse cup or drainage holes requires parents to manage water manually. Despite this, the tub’s simplicity is a strength for those who prefer streamlined setups. Users reported that the contoured design prevented babies from slipping, even when fully submerged, while the toddler end’s higher backrest provided better support for active older infants.  

## What We Like  
The EuroBath Plus shines in three key areas. First, its ergonomic design is a game-changer for newborns. A 3-month-old user’s parent noted how their baby tolerated baths longer in the reclined position, which reduced crying and made washing easier. Second, the dual-end system is a cost-effective longevity feature: one parent switched from the newborn end at 4 months to the toddler end at 12 months, stretching the tub’s use for over a year. Third, the $25 price tag is hard to beat. Compared to premium ergonomic tubs (which start at $50+), the EuroBath Plus offers similar anatomical benefits without breaking the bank. For example, a family with two children could invest $25 each time, avoiding the need for a single high-end tub. The stability of the non-tip base also stood out in real-world use—a 20-month-old was able to sit up and splash freely without the risk of tipping.  

## Drawbacks  
The EuroBath Plus is not without its limitations. Its large footprint (25.6” x 17.7”) makes it challenging to fit in single-bowl bathroom sinks, forcing many parents to use it in bathtubs or large kitchen sinks. A user in a small apartment noted this limited their ability to bathe their baby in the kitchen, requiring a trip to the bathroom for every use. Second, the lack of built-in accessories—such as a rinse cup or drainage plug—is a hassle. Parents must purchase or improvise these, adding time and effort to bath routines. Third, the non-removable insert complicates cleaning. While the smooth plastic is easy to wipe down, deep cleaning requires disassembling the entire unit, which is less convenient than removable parts in other models. These issues may not deter budget-conscious buyers, but they’re worth considering for those with space constraints or a preference for integrated features.  

## Safe Use & Setup  
Proper setup and safety practices are critical for maximizing the EuroBath Plus’s benefits. Begin by placing the tub in a stable, non-slip surface—preferably a double-bowl kitchen or bathroom sink. Ensure the non-tip base is fully seated and the textured feet are in contact with the sink’s surface. Avoid using it in small, single-bowl sinks due to its large size. For added stability, place a rubber bath mat or silicone grip under the tub to prevent sliding.  

When assembling, double-check that all parts are securely connected. The non-removable insert should fit snugly into the main body without gaps. Never submerge the tub beyond its 4.5-inch depth limit, as this could compromise stability or cause water overflow. Always supervise your baby during use—never leave them unattended, even for a moment.  

For newborns, position them in the fully reclined newborn end with their head supported by the elevated contour. As they grow, transition to the toddler end when they can sit upright independently (typically around 6 months). To prevent splashing water out, use a small waterproof tray placed near the sink to collect excess liquid. Finally, clean the tub after every use with a mild soap and soft cloth, focusing on the non-removable insert to avoid mold buildup.

## Buying Guide  
When choosing a baby bathtub, prioritize ergonomic design, safety features, and adaptability to your home’s layout. Anatomical correctness is crucial: look for reclined positions that support a baby’s spine rather than flat platforms. Non-tip bases are non-negotiable, especially as babies grow more active. The EuroBath Plus is ideal for parents whose sinks or bathtubs have ample space (a double-bowl kitchen sink works best). Avoid single-end tubs for newborns, as they become obsolete quickly. BPA-free materials are essential for health, and modular designs with removable parts simplify cleaning. A common mistake is underestimating the importance of depth: 4.5 inches is optimal for warmth and coverage, but not so deep as to submerge a baby’s chest. If you have a small bathroom, consider compact alternatives like wall-mounted tubs. For multi-child families, dual-end systems like the EuroBath Plus offer better long-term value. Always test the tub’s stability with your child’s weight in mind—some models tip easily with older toddlers.
