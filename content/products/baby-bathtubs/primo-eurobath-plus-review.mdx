## Primo EuroBath Plus: Budget Ergonomic Design That Lasts Years

The **Primo EuroBath Plus Baby Bathtub** is a budget-friendly, ergonomic solution for parents seeking a safe, anatomically correct bathing experience for newborns to toddlers. Designed for easy one-handed use, it supports babies in a natural reclined position that reduces neck strain and adapts to growing needs with two distinct ends. Ideal for families prioritizing comfort, longevity, and a no-frills approach to infant care.

## Design & Build

The EuroBath Plus is constructed from durable, BPA-free plastic, ensuring safety and resilience for daily use. Its 4.5-inch depth retains water temperature better than flatter alternatives, while the non-tip base—an essential safety feature—prevents accidental tipping during use. The tub's dual ends are its standout design element: the newborn end (for 0–6 months) features a fully reclined contour with elevated head support, while the toddler end (for 6–24 months) offers a semi-upright seat with higher backrests.

Aesthetically, the tub has a minimalist, European-inspired look with smooth edges and a matte finish that resists slipping. At 25.6 inches long and 17.7 inches wide, it's compact enough for most bathtubs but too large for standard single-bowl sinks—a limitation to note. The non-removable insert, while simplifying assembly, requires thorough cleaning of the entire unit. Weighing just under 2 pounds, it's lightweight for handling but sturdy enough to support a 25-pound toddler. The design prioritizes function over flashy extras, making it a practical choice for parents seeking a no-hassle, long-lasting tub that will outlast single-use products.

## Performance

The EuroBath Plus excels in its core purpose: providing a secure, ergonomic bathing experience. The reclined newborn position mimics a baby's natural spinal curve, reducing strain on their developing neck muscles. This is a critical advantage over flat-tub designs, which can cause discomfort or instability. The dual-end system extends usability beyond 24 months, doubling its value compared to single-configuration tubs. The 4.5-inch depth proved ideal in testing: warm water stays hot longer than in shallow tubs, reducing the need for constant reheating.

The non-tip base, with its wide, textured feet, kept the tub stable even when a toddler shifted positions—a common issue with cheaper models. Despite the lack of a built-in rinse cup or drainage holes, the tub's simplicity is a strength for those who prefer streamlined setups. Users reported that the contoured design prevented babies from slipping, even when fully submerged, while the toddler end's higher backrest provided better support for active older infants.

## What We Like

**Ergonomic Design**: The natural recline position is a game-changer for newborns. A 3-month-old user's parent noted how their baby tolerated baths longer in the reclined position, which reduced crying and made washing easier. Pediatric research shows that supporting a baby's natural spinal curve reduces stress and makes bath time more comfortable.

**Dual-End System**: One parent switched from the newborn end at 4 months to the toddler end at 12 months, stretching the tub's use for over a year. The ability to flip the tub and continue using it through toddlerhood is genuinely valuable.

**Exceptional Value**: At $25, the price is hard to beat. Compared to premium ergonomic tubs (which start at $50+), the EuroBath Plus offers similar anatomical benefits without breaking the bank. For families with two children, you could invest $25 each time, avoiding the need for a single high-end tub.

**Stability**: The non-tip base stood out in real-world use—a 20-month-old was able to sit up and splash freely without the risk of tipping. Safety is paramount, and this feature delivers.

## Drawbacks

**Large Footprint**: At 25.6" x 17.7", it's challenging to fit in single-bowl bathroom sinks, forcing many parents to use it in bathtubs or large kitchen sinks. A user in a small apartment noted this limited their ability to bathe their baby in the kitchen, requiring a trip to the bathroom for every use.

**Lack of Accessories**: The absence of built-in accessories—such as a rinse cup or drainage plug—is a hassle. Parents must purchase or improvise these, adding time and effort to bath routines.

**Non-Removable Insert**: Deep cleaning requires disassembling the entire unit, which is less convenient than removable parts in other models.

## Verdict

**Score: 8.2/10**

The Primo EuroBath Plus is a stellar choice for budget-conscious parents seeking ergonomic, long-lasting baby bathing. Its anatomically correct design, dual-end usability, and $25 price tag make it a standout in the budget category. Ideal for families with standard or large bathtubs, it's a no-brainer for those prioritizing comfort and value. However, skip this if you have a small bathroom sink or need integrated accessories. For parents who prefer a classic, no-frills approach, the EuroBath Plus delivers exceptional performance for the price.
