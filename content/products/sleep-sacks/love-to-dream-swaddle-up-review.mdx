**The Love To Dream Swaddle UP Original** redefines comfort for babies who resist traditional swaddles. Designed for newborns to 6-month-olds, this mid-range sleep sack ($35) stands out with its arms-up positioning and zip-off sleeves. Parents rave about its womb-like design and seamless transition to sleep sacks, earning it a 9/10 score. Ideal for babies who struggle with arms-down swaddles, it’s a game-changer for peaceful sleep without the struggle.

## Design & Build  
The Swaddle UP is crafted from 100% cotton jersey, a breathable, stretchy fabric that feels soft against sensitive baby skin. Its XS–L sizing accommodates weights from 5–22 lbs, though parents note sizing between weights can be tricky. The arms-up design mimics the fetal position, with a snug fit that prevents excess fabric from pooling around the face—a critical safety feature. The TOG ratings (0.2, 1.0, 2.5) allow for temperature regulation, with heavier TOG options ideal for colder environments. The sleeves are full-length, zipping off easily for transition. Aesthetics are minimalist, with muted colors and no frills, prioritizing function over flair. The material stretches to allow natural movement, reducing the restrictive “cocoon” feel of basic swaddles. Machine-washable and durable, it’s a practical choice for parents who value ease of care. While not the softest fabric compared to premium organic cotton swaddles, its stretch and breathability make it a favorite for active sleepers.

## Performance  
The Swaddle UP excels at its core purpose: aiding babies who reject arms-down swaddles. The arms-up position aligns with their in-utero posture, reducing the startle reflex and promoting deeper sleep. Parents report immediate success with babies who previously screamed through traditional swaddles. The snug, wraparound design prevents the fabric from sagging, which could otherwise cover the face—especially crucial for newborns. The zip-off sleeves are its standout feature: zipping off one sleeve for a week, then both, allows a gradual transition to a sleep sack. This method minimizes sleep disruption, with babies adjusting smoothly. The TOG ratings ensure year-round usability, though parents must choose carefully based on room temperature (0.2 for warm, 2.5 for cold). The stretchy fabric moves with the baby, allowing natural arm flailing without breaking the swaddle. For families struggling with swaddling wars, this design offers a practical solution that balances security with freedom.

## What We Like  
The arms-up design is a revelation for babies who reject traditional swaddles. One parent shared how their 6-week-old, who previously fought any arms-down wrap, slept through the night in the Swaddle UP. The zip-off sleeves ease the transition to a sleep sack, eliminating the abrupt switch that often disrupts sleep. For example, a parent began zipping off one sleeve during daytime naps, then both at night, allowing their baby to adjust over weeks. The snug fit also prevents the common issue of loose fabric around the face, a safety concern with cheaper swaddles. Another parent praised the TOG versatility, using the 2.5 rating in winter and 0.2 in summer. The sleeves’ ease of removal is a bonus for nighttime diaper changes, as parents can unzip a sleeve without fully unwrapping the baby. These features combine to create a product that’s as functional as it is innovative.

## Drawbacks  
While the Swaddle UP is a breakthrough for some, it’s not universally ideal. Babies who prefer arms-down swaddles may find the arms-up position uncomfortable, leading to resistance instead of relief. Parents of such babies may need to try standard swaddles instead. The $35 price tag is notably higher than basic cotton swaddles (often $10–$15), which could deter budget-conscious families. Though its quality justifies the cost for many, it’s still an investment. Sizing between weights can also be tricky—parents report needing to size up earlier than expected for 7–10 lb babies, as the XS fits only up to 7 lbs. This can lead to overpaying for a larger size or the product feeling loose too soon. Additionally, the arms-up design isn’t suitable for babies with reflux or those who need their arms down for medical reasons. For these cases, alternative swaddles or sleep sacks may be more appropriate.

## Safe Use & Setup  
Safety is paramount when using the Swaddle UP. Start by ensuring the room temperature is appropriate for the TOG rating chosen—avoid overdressing in warm weather. To set up, lie the baby on their back and gently pull the Swaddle UP over their head, ensuring the arms are snug in the sleeves. The arms-up position should feel secure but not constrictive; check for gaps where fabric might slip. For newborns, the fit should be tight enough to prevent rolling but loose enough to allow hip flexion and movement. Parents should always place the baby on their back to sleep and avoid adding extra blankets. When transitioning to sleep sacks, begin by zipping off one sleeve during the day, then both at night, to avoid sleep disruption. Never use the Swaddle UP with a hood, as loose fabric can pose a suffocation risk. Regularly inspect zippers for durability and replace if fraying. For added safety, avoid using the swaddle if the baby shows signs of rolling independently (typically after 4 months), as loose sleeves may pose a hazard.

The Swaddle UP’s TOG ratings also give it an edge over one-size-fits-all swaddles like the Boba Wrap, which may require additional layering for temperature control. While alternatives like the Moby Wrap offer flexibility for carriers, the Swaddle UP’s focus on sleep-specific design makes it a targeted solution for swaddling struggles.
