The **Maxi-Cosi Pria All-in-One** brings European car seat engineering philosophy to the US market — and what that means in practice is superior side-impact protection combined with thoughtful ergonomic details.

## Air Protect: The Side-Impact Advantage

Most car seat safety testing focuses on frontal collisions, but side impacts are increasingly common and disproportionately dangerous. Maxi-Cosi's Air Protect system uses air-cushioned headrest wings that deflect and absorb energy during side impacts more effectively than conventional foam padding.

This is the One4Life's weak point relative to the Pria — Britax's SafeCell is excellent for frontal protection but the Pria's Air Protect is the class leader for side impacts.

## The Adjustable Leg Rest

When rear-facing, newborns and toddlers have their legs floating in front of them. The Pria's 3-position leg rest extends to provide a comfortable footrest at the appropriate angle for the child's size. This prevents the awkward tucked-leg position that can restrict circulation and cause fussiness on long drives.

## Four-in-One Configuration

1. **Rear-facing infant** with 5-point harness (4–40 lbs)
2. **Forward-facing with harness** (22–65 lbs)
3. **High-back booster** (30–100 lbs)
4. **Backless booster** positioning in some configurations

This range covers most children from the hospital through approximately age 10–12.

## Safety Standards & Certifications

Car seats sold in the United States must comply with **FMVSS 213** (Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard No. 213), which governs dynamic crash performance, harness strength, and flammability. Look for **JPMA certification** as an additional quality marker. All infant car seats are also tested against **ASTM F2050**. The safest installation method is always LATCH or a correctly tightened seat belt — never both simultaneously unless the manufacturer explicitly permits it.

The **Maxi-Cosi Pria All-in-One** scores 8.8/10 — safety standards compliance is factored into this rating and was verified against current CPSC and ASTM requirements at time of review.
