The **DaVinci Kalani 4-in-1 Convertible Crib** is the crib we recommend when parents ask: "I want a safe, convertible crib without spending $600+." It delivers on every important metric at a fraction of luxury crib prices.

## GREENGUARD Gold: Why It Matters

Babies sleep 14+ hours per day. The crib is the most sustained chemical exposure environment in a baby's life. GREENGUARD Gold certification means independent laboratory testing has verified that the Kalani meets Children & Schools standards for chemical emissions — stricter than standard GREENGUARD.

In practical terms: no harmful off-gassing of VOCs, formaldehyde, or other chemicals near your newborn's sleeping face.

## Four-in-One Longevity

The Kalani starts as a standard crib with 3-position mattress height. As your child grows:

1. **Crib** — birth to approximately 18–24 months
2. **Toddler bed** — remove one side, add toddler rail (sold separately)
3. **Daybed** — open front configuration for older toddlers
4. **Full-size bed** — with conversion rails (sold separately), becomes an adult full-size bed

Buy once, keep forever — or until they leave for college.

## The Under-Crib Drawer

DaVinci sells a matching under-crib drawer that uses the space beneath the Kalani for blanket, sheet, and diaper storage. In a small nursery, this is genuinely valuable real estate.

## Safety Standards & Certifications

Cribs must meet **ASTM F1169** (full-size cribs) or **ASTM F406** (non-full-size cribs) and comply with **CPSC 16 CFR Parts 1219 and 1220**, which prohibit drop-side rails and mandate slat spacing of no more than 2⅜ inches. **GREENGUARD Gold** certification means the crib has been independently tested for over 360 chemical emissions — important for a product your baby sleeps in 12–16 hours daily.

## Parent Tips

- Budget for accessories upfront: Toddler rail and full-size rails sold separately. Add these to your cart at purchase to avoid paying extra shipping later.
- Assembly takes 60+ minutes — watch the official installation video online before starting, and allow more time than the estimate. A second person makes a significant difference.
- Assemble the crib before the baby arrives — assembling furniture with a newborn in the house is unexpectedly stressful.
- Lower the mattress to the lowest position before your baby can pull to stand — typically around 5–6 months.
