The **SNOO Smart Sleeper** is the most polarising product in baby gear. Parents either credit it with saving their sanity or wonder how a $1,700 bassinet justified its price for six months of use.

## How It Works

The SNOO uses a microphone to detect crying. When your baby fusses, it automatically responds by increasing swaying motion and white noise intensity across three escalating levels. If the baby settles, it reduces back to a baseline rocking motion. If crying continues at maximum response, an alert is sent to the parent app.

The key innovation is the SNOO Sack — a built-in swaddle that clips to the side rail, keeping the baby safely on their back throughout the night. This is what earned the AAP's endorsement: unlike loose swaddles that can unwrap, the SNOO Sack keeps the sleeping position safe.

## Sleep Improvement: Realistic Expectations

The SNOO is most effective for the fourth trimester (weeks 1–12). During this period, the continuous motion mimics the womb environment and many babies who would wake after 45 minutes complete another sleep cycle.

Clinical data shows average improvement of 1–2 hours per night. Not 4 hours. If you're hoping for a full night's sleep from week 2, adjust expectations.

## The 6-Month Limit

The SNOO is a bassinet, not a crib. Once your baby tries to sit up — typically around 5–6 months — the SNOO is retired. The per-day cost at purchase price works out to approximately $9/day.

## Our Verdict

Rent the SNOO if you can. Six months of meaningful sleep improvement is genuinely life-changing during one of parenting's hardest stretches. The purchase price is harder to justify, but the resale market is strong.
