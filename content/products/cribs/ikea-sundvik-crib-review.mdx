The **IKEA SUNDVIK Crib** answers the question that many first-time parents ask: do I really need to spend $300+ on a crib? For many families, the answer is no — and the SUNDVIK delivers safe, functional sleep space at the lowest credible price point.

## Safety First: Yes, It's Safe

Every crib sold in the US must pass ASTM safety standards and every European crib must pass EN 716 standards. The SUNDVIK passes both. Slat spacing meets requirements (no wider than 2.375 inches), structural integrity is tested, and material standards are met. This is not a safety compromise — it's a materials and features compromise.

What the SUNDVIK lacks is GREENGUARD Gold certification, which provides an additional layer of chemical emissions testing. For parents concerned about VOCs, this is worth knowing. For parents where budget is the primary driver, the SUNDVIK is a legitimate safe choice.

## IKEA Mattress Requirement

The most important SUNDVIK caveat: it requires an IKEA-sized mattress (27.5 x 52 inches). Standard US crib mattresses are slightly wider (28 x 52 inches) and may leave gaps that are unsafe. Always use either an IKEA mattress or a mattress sold specifically as "IKEA compatible." IKEA's PLUTTEN, VYSSA, and LENAST mattresses are designed for the SUNDVIK.

## Clean Design

The white SUNDVIK has clean, minimal Scandinavian lines. It doesn't try to be ornate — which means it complements almost any nursery decor. The 2-position mattress height accommodates newborns (high position) and mobile infants who might try to climb out (low position).
