The Babyletto Hudson has been one of the most popular cribs in America for several years — and for good reason. It hits the sweet spot of design, safety, sustainability, and value that few competitors achieve.

## First Impressions: The Design

The Hudson's appeal starts with its looks. The clean, modern lines with spindle sides and rounded edges create a timeless aesthetic that works equally well in Scandi-minimal nurseries and more traditional rooms. Available in a range of finishes — white, washed natural, grey, and more — there's a colour for virtually every design scheme.

The GREENGUARD Gold certification means the finish has been tested for over 360 VOC chemicals and is safe for use around babies from day one. No off-gassing concerns, no 48-hour "airing out" period.

## Build Quality

The Hudson is made from sustainable New Zealand pine — a hardwood that feels solid and substantial when assembled. There's no wobbling or flexing, and the slat spacing (2.375 inches) meets all US and UK safety standards, preventing head entrapment.

The hardware is all metal, not plastic — which means it won't strip and it holds up to years of use.

## Converting: How the 3-in-1 Works

The Hudson comes with a toddler bed conversion rail included in the box (many competitor cribs charge extra for this):

1. **Crib stage:** Standard crib with three mattress height positions. Use the highest for a newborn, lower as they start pulling to stand.
2. **Toddler bed:** Remove one side rail, attach the included toddler bed conversion rail. Suitable when your child starts climbing out (usually 2–3 years).
3. **Daybed:** Remove the front rail entirely for a simple daybed/sofa configuration.

This gives you a useful item from birth to approximately age 5. The value calculation over that lifespan makes the $479 price feel very reasonable.

## Assembly

Allow 60–90 minutes for assembly and read the instructions fully before starting. The bolt placement can be confusing on first reading. The hardware quality is good — all tools needed are included. Recommended: two people for the final crib assembly step.

## What's Missing

The Hudson doesn't have an under-crib drawer — if under-crib storage matters to you, look at the DaVinci Kalani or similar. It also doesn't convert to a full-size bed, which some parents want for longevity beyond the toddler years.

## Our Verdict

At $469–$479, the Babyletto Hudson is exceptional value. GREENGUARD Gold certification, convertible design, premium construction, and genuinely beautiful aesthetics make it our top mid-range crib pick. It's the crib we recommend to most parents as a first choice.
